# CHAPTER 58. VEHICLES AND TRAFFIC

## ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

### Sec. 58-1. Definitions.

Words and phrases used in this chapter shall have the same meanings as those set out in G.S. 20-1 et seq., except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.

### Sec. 58-2. Applicability.

The provisions of this chapter shall apply to all vehicles, pushcarts, tractors, trailers, bicycles, and animals on public streets and ways within the corporate limits of the town.

### Sec. 58-3. Public employees to obey traffic regulations.

The provisions of this chapter applicable to the operation of vehicles shall apply to the operators of all vehicles owned or operated by the United States, any state, or any county, city, or political subdivision, subject to such specific exceptions as are set forth in this chapter.

### Sec. 58-4. Authority of police and fire officials.

(a) It shall be the duty of the officers of the police department and such officers as are assigned by the chief of police to enforce the street traffic regulations of the town and all of the state vehicle laws applicable to street traffic in the town.

(b) Officers of the fire department may direct or assist the police in directing traffic at or near fires.

### Sec. 58-5. Obedience to officials.

No person shall willfully fail or refuse to comply with any lawful order or direction of a police officer or fire official invested by law with authority to direct, control, or regulate traffic.

### Sec. 58-6. Official traffic map.

The town maintains an official traffic map on file at town hall indicating the following traffic control designations:

(1) Speed zones.

(2) Stop sign locations.

(3) Yield sign locations.

(4) One-way street designations.

(5) No-turn designations.

(6) Parking prohibitions — all times.

(7) Parking time limits.

(8) Loading zones.

(9) School zones.

(10) Other designated traffic control areas.

### Sec. 58-7. Coasters and roller skates restricted from roadway.

No person upon roller skates, or riding in or by means of any coaster, toy vehicle, or similar device, shall go upon any roadway except while crossing a street on a crosswalk.

### Sec. 58-8. Authorized emergency vehicles.

(a) The driver of an authorized emergency vehicle may:

(1) Park or stand, irrespective of the provisions of this chapter.

(2) Proceed past a red or stop signal or stop sign, but only after slowing down as may be necessary for safe operation.

(3) Exceed the maximum speed limits.

(4) Disregard regulations governing direction of movement.

(b) The exemptions granted by this section shall apply only when the driver of such vehicle is giving an audible signal by siren and displaying a flashing light visible from at least 500 feet.

(c) The foregoing provisions shall not relieve the driver of an authorized emergency vehicle from the duty to drive with due regard for the safety of all persons.

### Sec. 58-9. Trains blocking crossings.

No railroad train shall block a grade crossing for more than five minutes.

### Sec. 58-10. Clerk constituted traffic bureau.

(a) The clerk shall act as traffic violations bureau with authority to accept payment of fines and costs for the following violations:

(1) Speeding.

(2) Failure to stop at stop sign.

(3) Failure to yield right-of-way.

(4) Improper parking.

(5) Improper lane change.

(6) Following too closely.

(7) Failure to signal.

(8) Improper turn.

(9) Failure to stop for pedestrian.

(10) Other minor traffic violations as designated by the chief.

### Sec. 58-11. Penalties.

Penalties for violations of this chapter shall be as provided in section 1-6.

**Sec. 58-12. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE II. OPERATION

### Sec. 58-13. Emerging from alley or driveway.

The driver of a vehicle emerging from an alley, driveway, or building shall stop such vehicle immediately prior to driving onto a sidewalk or into the sidewalk area extending across any alleyway.

### Sec. 58-14. Stop when traffic obstructed.

No driver shall enter an intersection or a marked crosswalk unless there is sufficient space on the other side of the intersection or crosswalk to accommodate the vehicle being driven.

### Sec. 58-15. Speed limits.

The general speed limit within the corporate limits is 35 miles per hour unless otherwise posted.

### Sec. 58-16. Required turning positions.

All turns at intersections shall be made from the appropriate lane as required by the official traffic map and applicable state law.

### Sec. 58-17. Turning markers.

The town may mark lanes for turning movements.

### Sec. 58-18. Authority to place restricted turn signs.

The town manager is authorized to place signs prohibiting or restricting turns at any intersection.

### Sec. 58-19. Obedience to no-turn signs.

No person shall make a prohibited turn at any intersection where signs have been erected indicating that no turn is permitted.

### Sec. 58-20. No U-turns in business district.

No person shall make a U-turn in any business district.

### Sec. 58-21. Authority to designate one-way streets.

The town manager is authorized to designate any street within the corporate limits as a one-way street.

### Sec. 58-22. One-way streets.

On one-way streets all traffic shall move only in the indicated direction.

### Sec. 58-23. Boarding and alighting from public conveyances.

No person shall board or alight from any street car or bus while such vehicle is in motion.

### Sec. 58-24. Unlawful riding.

No person shall ride on any vehicle upon any portion of the vehicle not designed or intended for the use of passengers.

### Sec. 58-25. Jumping on vehicles without permission.

No person shall climb on or jump on any vehicle without the permission of the operator thereof.

### Sec. 58-26. No body protruding from vehicle.

No person shall ride with any body part outside a vehicle window or door in such a manner as to create a hazard.

### Sec. 58-27. Maximum three persons in front seat.

No more than three persons shall occupy the front seat of any motor vehicle.

### Sec. 58-28. No vehicles on sidewalks.

No person shall drive any vehicle upon or across any sidewalk, except at a permanent or temporary driveway.

### Sec. 58-29. No loitering on private property.

No person shall park, stand, or loiter in any vehicle on private property between 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. when the premises are posted with signs prohibiting such activity without the owner's permission.

**Secs. 58-30—58-46. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE III. TRAFFIC CONTROL DEVICES

### Sec. 58-47. Obedience to traffic control signs.

Failure to obey any official traffic control sign, signal, or marking erected pursuant to this chapter is a misdemeanor.

### Sec. 58-48. Signs as prerequisite to enforcement.

No provision of this chapter for which official traffic control devices are required shall be enforced against an alleged violator unless at the time and place of the alleged violation an official device was in proper position and sufficiently legible to be seen by an ordinarily observant person.

### Sec. 58-49. Conform to state specifications.

All traffic control devices shall conform to state specifications.

### Sec. 58-50. Unauthorized signs.

Any unofficial sign, signal, or marking placed or maintained in a manner as to interfere with traffic on any public street shall constitute a public nuisance and shall be removed without notice.

### Sec. 58-51. No interference with official devices.

No person shall without lawful authority attempt to or in fact alter, deface, injure, knock down, or remove any official traffic control device.

### Sec. 58-52. Authority to establish play streets.

The town manager is authorized to establish play streets during certain hours.

### Sec. 58-53. Traffic lanes.

The town manager is authorized to mark traffic lanes on streets.

### Sec. 58-54. Zone of quiet.

Within a zone of quiet, no person shall sound any horn or other warning device on a vehicle except in an emergency.

### Sec. 58-55. School zones.

School speed zones shall be designated on the official traffic map.

### Sec. 58-56. Yield signs.

Drivers approaching a yield sign shall slow down to a speed reasonable for the existing conditions, and shall yield the right-of-way to any vehicle in the intersection or approaching on another roadway so closely as to constitute an immediate hazard.

### Sec. 58-57. Authority to erect stop signs.

The town manager is authorized to erect stop signs at intersections.

### Sec. 58-58. Intersections where stop required.

Every driver approaching a stop sign shall stop at a clearly marked stop line, or if none, before entering the crosswalk on the near side of the intersection, or if none, then at the point nearest the intersecting roadway where the driver has a view of approaching traffic.

### Sec. 58-59. Stop signs.

Stop signs shall be at least eight inches in diameter and shall be illuminated or reflectorized for nighttime visibility.

### Sec. 58-60. Vehicles to stop at stop signs.

Every driver of a vehicle approaching a stop sign shall stop as required by section 58-58 and shall yield the right-of-way to any vehicle in the intersection or approaching on another roadway so closely as to constitute an immediate hazard during the time when the driver is moving across or within the intersection or junction of roadways.

### Sec. 58-61. Avoidance of traffic control devices prohibited.

No person shall operate a vehicle on private property to avoid compliance with any official traffic control device.

**Secs. 58-62—58-69. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE IV. PARKING, STOPPING AND STANDING

### DIVISION 1. RESERVED

**Secs. 58-70—58-78. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 2. REGULATIONS

### Sec. 58-79. Application of division.

The provisions of this division shall govern the stopping, standing, and parking of vehicles.

### Sec. 58-80. Regulations not exclusive.

The provisions of this division imposing a time limit on parking shall not relieve any person of the duty to observe other and more restrictive provisions prohibiting or limiting the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles in specified places or at specified times.

### Sec. 58-81. Parking signs required.

No parking regulations shall be enforceable unless official signs are in place indicating the regulation.

### Sec. 58-82. No stopping in streets.

No vehicle shall stop, stand, or park in a street in such a manner or under such conditions as to impede the normal and reasonable movement of traffic, except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic or in compliance with the directions of a police officer or official traffic control device.

### Sec. 58-83. Prohibited stopping, standing, or parking locations.

No person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle at the following locations:

(1) On a sidewalk.

(2) In front of a public or private driveway.

(3) Within an intersection.

(4) Within 15 feet of a fire hydrant.

(5) On a crosswalk.

(6) Within 20 feet of a crosswalk at an intersection.

(7) Within 30 feet upon the approach to any flashing beacon, stop sign, yield sign, or traffic control signal at an intersection.

(8) Within 50 feet of the nearest rail of a railroad crossing.

(9) Within 20 feet of the driveway entrance to any fire station and on the side of a street opposite the entrance to any fire station within 75 feet of said entrance.

(10) Alongside or opposite any street excavation or obstruction when stopping, standing, or parking would obstruct traffic.

(11) On the roadway side of any vehicle stopped or parked at the edge or curb of a street.

(12) Upon any bridge or other elevated structure upon a highway or within a highway tunnel.

(13) At any place where official signs prohibit stopping.

(14) At any place where signs prohibit standing or parking.

### Sec. 58-84. No obstruction of roadway less than ten feet wide.

No person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle so as to leave available less than ten feet of the width of a roadway for the free movement of vehicular traffic.

### Sec. 58-85. Alley parking.

(a) No person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle within an alley in such a position as to block the driveway entrance to any abutting property, or in such a manner as to leave available less than ten feet of the width of the alley for the free movement of vehicular traffic.

(b) No person shall park a vehicle to block any driveway.

### Sec. 58-86. Prohibited parking.

No person shall park a vehicle:

(1) For the principal purpose of displaying it for sale.

(2) For the purpose of washing, greasing, or repairing such vehicle, except repairs necessitated by an emergency.

(3) For the principal purpose of storage, or as a trailer without a tow vehicle attached.

(4) With a detached trailer.

(5) With a tractor-trailer combination unattended for more than 24 consecutive hours.

### Sec. 58-87. All-night parking prohibited.

No vehicle shall be parked on any street between 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. as designated on the official traffic map.

### Sec. 58-88. No parking adjacent to schools.

No vehicle shall be parked during school hours in any area designated as a no-parking zone adjacent to any school.

### Sec. 58-89. Standing on one-way roadways.

On one-way roadways, standing may be permitted on either side of the roadway unless official signs indicate otherwise.

### Sec. 58-90. Near hazardous or congested places.

No person shall stop, stand, or park any vehicle in any area designated as hazardous or congested.

### Sec. 58-91. No parking for advertising.

No person shall stop, stand, or park any vehicle for the principal purpose of advertising.

### Sec. 58-92. Loading and unloading zones.

No person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle for any purpose or length of time in any loading zone other than for the expeditious loading or unloading of passengers or merchandise.

### Sec. 58-93. Within 18 inches of curb.

No person shall park a vehicle unless the right-hand wheels of such vehicle are within 18 inches of the right-hand curb or edge of the roadway.

### Sec. 58-94. Angle parking signs.

The town manager is authorized to designate angle parking zones on any roadway.

### Sec. 58-95. Angle parking markings.

Where angle parking is permitted, drivers shall park at the angle indicated by the official markings.

### Sec. 58-96. Lights on parked vehicles outside business or residence district at night.

Any vehicle parked or stopped on a roadway outside a business or residence district at nighttime shall display appropriate lights visible from 500 feet to the front and rear.

### Sec. 58-97. Vehicles backed to curb.

No person shall back a vehicle to any curb except when loading or unloading merchandise.

### Sec. 58-98. No running engine while parked.

No person shall allow the engine of a parked vehicle to run continuously for more than five minutes.

### Sec. 58-99. Moving cars from parked positions.

No person shall move a vehicle not lawfully under his control.

### Sec. 58-100. Passenger loading zones.

In any passenger loading zone between 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m., no driver shall stop a vehicle for longer than 10 minutes.

### Sec. 58-101. Commercial loading zones.

In any commercial loading zone between 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m., no driver shall stop a vehicle for longer than 30 minutes.

### Sec. 58-102. Public carrier stops.

The town manager is authorized to establish bus stops on streets and highways.

### Sec. 58-103. Public carrier stands.

The town manager is authorized to establish taxicab stands on streets and highways.

### Sec. 58-104. Restricted use of bus and taxicab stands.

No person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle other than a bus in a bus stop, or other than a taxicab in a taxicab stand.

### Sec. 58-105. Parking prohibited at all times on certain streets.

Parking is prohibited at all times on streets designated on the official traffic map.

### Sec. 58-106. Parking time limited on certain streets.

Parking is limited to the time periods designated on the official traffic map on designated streets.

### Sec. 58-107. No parking with left side to curb.

No person shall park a vehicle with the left side of such vehicle nearest the curb except on one-way streets.

### Sec. 58-108. Towing of standing or parked vehicles.

A law enforcement officer may order the removal and towing of a vehicle under the following circumstances:

(1) The vehicle has been reported stolen.

(2) The vehicle is obstructing a bridge or intersection.

(3) The vehicle appears to be abandoned.

(4) The vehicle is incapacitated and creating a traffic hazard.

(5) The vehicle is illegally parked creating a safety hazard.

(6) The vehicle has been continuously parked in one place for more than 48 hours.

(7) The driver has been arrested.

(8) The removal is necessary to protect the public safety.

(9) The vehicle is parked in violation of emergency parking restrictions.

**Secs. 58-109—58-115. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 3. PARKING OF SPECIAL AND COMMERCIAL VEHICLES

### Sec. 58-116. No oversized commercial vehicles on streets or in residential areas.

(a) No person shall park or leave standing an oversized commercial vehicle on any street or on any parcel in a residential area of less than one acre.

(b) Exceptions:

(1) Temporary parking while actively loading or unloading.

(2) Emergency vehicles.

(3) Utility service vehicles while performing service.

(4) Construction vehicles at active construction sites.

(5) Delivery vehicles making deliveries.

(6) Vehicles authorized by the town.

(7) Vehicles at locations zoned for commercial or industrial use.

### Sec. 58-117. Oversized commercial vehicle defined.

An oversized commercial vehicle is any vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating of more than 26,000 pounds, or any vehicle with more than two axles, or any vehicle required to display a hazardous materials placard.

### Sec. 58-118. Enforcement.

Enforcement of the provisions of this division shall be by the China Grove Police Department.

### Sec. 58-119. Penalty.

Violations of this division shall be subject to penalties as provided in section 1-6.

**Secs. 58-120—58-127. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE V. PEDESTRIANS

### Sec. 58-128. Pedestrians subject to traffic control signals.

Pedestrians shall obey the instructions of official traffic control signals applicable to them.

### Sec. 58-129. Pedestrians' right-of-way in crosswalks.

(a) When traffic control signals are not in place or in operation the driver of a vehicle shall stop to yield the right-of-way to a pedestrian crossing the roadway within a crosswalk.

(b) No pedestrian shall suddenly leave a curb or other place of safety and walk or run into the path of a vehicle which is so close as to constitute an immediate hazard.

### Sec. 58-130. Pedestrians to use right half of crosswalk.

Pedestrians shall move in the right half of crosswalks.

### Sec. 58-131. Crossing at right angles.

No pedestrian shall cross a roadway at any place other than in a crosswalk except by crossing at right angles to the nearest crosswalk.

### Sec. 58-132. When pedestrian shall yield.

Every pedestrian crossing a roadway at any point other than within a marked crosswalk or within an unmarked crosswalk at an intersection shall yield the right-of-way to all vehicles.

### Sec. 58-133. Walking along roadways.

(a) Where sidewalks are provided, it shall be unlawful for any pedestrian to walk along and upon an adjacent roadway.

(b) Where sidewalks are not provided, any pedestrian walking along and upon a highway shall walk only on the shoulder, as far from the edge of the roadway as practicable, and, if on a two-lane roadway, shall walk only on the left side of the roadway or its shoulder facing traffic which may approach from the opposite direction.

### Sec. 58-134. No soliciting rides or business.

No person shall stand on a highway for the purpose of soliciting a ride, employment, or business from the occupant of any vehicle.

### Sec. 58-135. Drivers to exercise due care.

Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, every driver of a vehicle shall exercise due care to avoid colliding with any pedestrian.

**Secs. 58-136—58-150. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE VI. BICYCLES

### Sec. 58-151. Effect of regulations.

It is a misdemeanor for any person to do any act forbidden or to fail to perform any act required by this article.

### Sec. 58-152. Traffic laws apply.

Every person riding a bicycle upon a roadway shall be granted all the rights and shall be subject to all the duties applicable to the driver of a vehicle under this chapter.

### Sec. 58-153. Obedience to traffic control devices.

Any person operating a bicycle shall obey the instructions of official traffic control signals, signs, and other control devices applicable to vehicles.

### Sec. 58-154. Riding on permanent seat and capacity.

No person operating a bicycle shall allow a person to ride other than upon a permanent and regular seat attached to the bicycle.

### Sec. 58-155. Riding on roadways.

(a) Every person operating a bicycle upon a roadway shall ride as near to the right side of the roadway as practicable.

(b) Persons riding bicycles shall not ride more than two abreast.

(c) Where a usable bicycle path has been provided adjacent to the roadway, bicycle riders shall use such path.

### Sec. 58-156. Speed.

No person shall operate a bicycle at a speed greater than is reasonable and prudent.

### Sec. 58-157. Emerging from alley or driveway.

The operator of a bicycle emerging from an alley or driveway shall, upon approaching a sidewalk or the sidewalk area extending across any alleyway, yield the right-of-way to all pedestrians.

### Sec. 58-158. One hand on handlebars when carrying articles.

No person operating a bicycle shall carry any package, bundle, or article which prevents the operator from keeping at least one hand upon the handlebars.

### Sec. 58-159. Parking.

No person shall park a bicycle upon a street in any manner other than against the curb, in a designated bicycle rack, or against a building.

### Sec. 58-160. Skateboards and bicycles in business district.

(a) No person shall ride a bicycle or skateboard in any business district.

(b) No person shall ride a bicycle on any sidewalk between 7:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.

(c) Violation is a misdemeanor.

(d) A juvenile in violation shall be adjudicated delinquent; any other person shall be fined not more than $100.00.

### Sec. 58-161. Lights and brakes.

(a) Every bicycle operated on a highway during darkness shall be equipped with a lamp on the front which shall emit a white light visible from a distance of at least 500 feet to the front.

(b) Every bicycle shall be equipped with a reflector on the rear that is visible from 50 to 300 feet to the rear.

(c) Every bicycle shall be equipped with a brake which will enable the operator to make the braked wheel skid on dry, level, clean pavement.

**Secs. 58-162—58-169. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE VII. LOW SPEED VEHICLES

### Sec. 58-170. Definitions.

(a) *Cart* means a vehicle operated at a maximum speed of 28 miles per hour that is not a lawn mower, golf cart used on a golf course, ATV, or similar device.

(b) *Low speed vehicle (LSV)* means a four-wheeled vehicle, other than a truck, with a maximum speed greater than 20 miles per hour but not more than 28 miles per hour.

(c) *Permit of operation* means a sticker issued by the town and affixed to the left side of the vehicle.

### Sec. 58-171. Rules of operation.

Carts and low speed vehicles shall be operated in accordance with the following rules:

(1) Liability insurance is required.

(2) The operator shall be liable for all damages resulting from operation.

(3) The operator shall be at least 16 years of age and possess a valid driver's license.

(4) Operation is permitted during daylight hours only, unless the vehicle is equipped with lights.

(5) Operation during inclement weather is prohibited.

(6) The vehicle shall be equipped with a rear vision mirror and an LSV placard conforming to ASAE Standard S276.4.

(7) Operation is permitted on US 29/601 and NC 152 only for crossing or access purposes; continuous travel on these state highways is prohibited.

(8) The operator shall comply with all NC traffic laws.

(9) The operator shall yield to pedestrians and overtaking vehicles.

(10) All passengers shall be seated.

(11) The vehicle shall not exceed manufacturer's stated passenger capacity.

(12) Operation on sidewalks is prohibited.

(13) Parking in handicapped spaces is prohibited without a valid placard.

### Sec. 58-172. Permit application.

(a) Initial permit fees shall not exceed $50.00.

(b) Annual renewal fees are $10.00; permits run January 1 through December 31.

(c) Applications shall include driver's license information, proof of insurance, and a release and indemnity agreement.

(d) Permits may be revoked for misrepresentation, insurance revocation, or unsafe operation.

(e) The permittee shall have 15 days to request a hearing upon notice of revocation.

### Sec. 58-173. Reciprocity.

The town shall recognize valid low speed vehicle permits issued by other municipalities within Rowan County.

### Sec. 58-174. Penalty.

Violations of this article shall be subject to a civil penalty of $50.00 as provided in section 1-6.
