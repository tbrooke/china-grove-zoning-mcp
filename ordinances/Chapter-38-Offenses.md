# CHAPTER 38. OFFENSES

## ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

**Secs. 38-1—38-7. Reserved.**

### Sec. 38-8. Security alarm systems.

(a) A permit is required to operate a security alarm system within the town. The permit fee is $5.00.

(b) False alarm fee schedule:

| False Alarms | Police Fee | Fire Fee |
|---|---|---|
| 5th and 6th | $25.00 | $50.00 |
| 7th and 8th | $50.00 | $100.00 |
| 9th and 10th | $100.00 | $200.00 |
| Each thereafter | Double previous | Double previous |

(c) Any alarm system generating more than ten false alarms in a 12-month period shall require a state-licensed inspection of the alarm system.

(d) Audible alarms must automatically reset within ten minutes after activation. An alarm that fails to reset within ten minutes shall be deemed a false alarm and subject to a fee of $25.00.

### Sec. 38-9. Campaign signs on state highway system roads.

(a) Campaign signs may be placed along state highway system roads during the period beginning 30 days before the start of early voting and ending ten days after the primary or election day.

(b) Sign placement rules:

(1) Signs shall not be placed in the right-of-way of any controlled-access highway.

(2) Signs shall be placed at least three feet from the edge of the pavement.

(3) Signs shall not obstruct the view at any intersection.

(4) Signs shall not exceed 42 inches in height.

(5) Signs shall not exceed 864 square inches in area.

(6) Signs shall not obscure other signs.

(c) The unlawful removal of a campaign sign is a Class 3 misdemeanor.

**Secs. 38-10—38-32. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE II. WEAPONS

### Sec. 38-33. Restrictions.

No person shall carry or possess any firearm or deadly weapon in or upon any public building, public grounds, or public park owned or controlled by the town. This prohibition does not apply to:

(1) Members of the United States Armed Forces while in the performance of their official duties.

(2) Law enforcement officers while in the performance of their official duties.

(3) Town employees authorized to carry firearms in the performance of their duties.

(4) Persons possessing a valid concealed carry permit issued pursuant to G.S. 14-415.11.

### Sec. 38-34. Dangerous weapons in public.

(a) *Definitions.* The following weapons are defined as dangerous weapons for purposes of this section:

*Billy club* means a short stick or club used as a weapon.

*Nunchaku* means an oriental weapon consisting of two sticks connected by a chain or cord.

*Star* means a pointed metal throwing weapon.

*Switchblade* means a knife with a blade that opens automatically by the force of a spring.

(b) It shall be unlawful for any person to carry, possess, or have on his person any of the weapons defined in this section while going upon public streets, public ways, or any premises open to the public.

(c) Law enforcement officers are authorized to confiscate any dangerous weapon carried in violation of this section.

### Sec. 38-35. Discharge of firearms.

(a) It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge any firearm within the corporate limits of the town, except in self-defense.

(b) The discharge of BB guns, pellet guns, bows, or crossbows on private property with the permission of the property owner is permitted.

### Sec. 38-36. Weapons on town property.

(a) It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or carry any weapon on town property.

(b) Exceptions: law enforcement officers, ROTC members, and persons with valid concealed carry permits.

(c) Penalty: a fine not to exceed $500.00 or imprisonment not to exceed six months.

## ARTICLE III. JUVENILE CURFEW

### Sec. 38-71. Purpose.

The purpose of this article is to promote the general welfare and protect the general public through the reduction of juvenile violence and crime within the town.

### Sec. 38-72. Definitions.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

*Direct route* means the most geographically direct route between two places.

*Emergency* means an unforeseen combination of circumstances or the resulting state that calls for immediate action. The term includes, but is not limited to, a fire, a natural disaster, an automobile accident, or any situation requiring immediate action to prevent serious bodily injury or loss of life.

*Establishment* means any privately owned place of business operated for a profit to which the public is invited, including but not limited to any place of amusement or entertainment.

*Guardian* means a person who, under court order, is the guardian of the person of a juvenile or a public or private agency with whom a juvenile has been placed by a court.

*Juvenile* means any person under the age of 16 years.

*Owner or operator* means any individual, firm, association, partnership or corporation operating, managing or conducting any establishment.

*Parent* means a person who is a natural parent, adoptive parent, or step-parent of a juvenile.

*Public place* means any place to which the public or a substantial group of the public has access and includes, but is not limited to, streets, highways, and the common areas of schools, hospitals, apartment houses, office buildings, transport facilities, and shops.

*Remain* means to linger or stay, or fail to leave premises when requested to do so by a police officer or the owner, operator, or other person in control of the premises.

*Restricted hours* means:
- Sunday through Thursday: 11:00 p.m. until 6:00 a.m. of the following day.
- Friday and Saturday: 12:01 a.m. until 6:00 a.m.

### Sec. 38-73. Applicability.

This article applies to all juveniles within the corporate limits of the town.

### Sec. 38-74. Violations.

The following shall constitute violations of this article:

(1) A juvenile remaining in any public place during restricted hours.

(2) A parent or guardian knowingly permitting a juvenile to remain in any public place during restricted hours.

(3) An owner or operator of an establishment knowingly allowing a juvenile to remain on the premises during restricted hours.

(4) Any person who aids or abets a juvenile in violating this article.

(5) Any person who refuses to surrender a juvenile to a law enforcement officer.

### Sec. 38-75. Exceptions.

The provisions of this article shall not apply when a juvenile is:

(1) Accompanied by the juvenile's parent or guardian.

(2) Accompanied by an adult 18 years of age or older who has been authorized by the juvenile's parent or guardian to be with the juvenile.

(3) On an errand at the direction of the juvenile's parent or guardian, taking a direct route, and the errand is completed before 12:30 a.m.

(4) In a motor vehicle involved in interstate travel.

(5) Traveling with adult supervision in a motor vehicle.

(6) Engaged in lawful employment activity or traveling by direct route to or from such employment.

(7) Involved in an emergency.

(8) On the sidewalk abutting the juvenile's residence or abutting the residence of a next-door neighbor, if the neighbor did not complain to the police department about the juvenile's presence.

(9) Attending an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by a school, civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the juvenile, or going to or returning from such an activity by direct route.

(10) Exercising First Amendment rights protected by the United States Constitution.

(11) Married or emancipated.

(12) In possession of a special permit issued by the chief of police.

### Sec. 38-76. Defense.

It is a defense to prosecution that the owner or operator of an establishment promptly notified the police department that a juvenile was present on the premises during restricted hours and refused to leave.

### Sec. 38-77. Enforcement.

Before taking any enforcement action under this article, a law enforcement officer shall ask the apparent offender's age and reason for being in the public place during restricted hours. The officer shall not issue a citation or make an arrest unless the officer reasonably concludes that an offense has occurred and no exception applies.

### Sec. 38-78. Penalties.

(a) A juvenile who violates this article shall be adjudicated as a delinquent.

(b) Any other person who violates this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be fined not more than $100.00, pursuant to G.S. 14-4.
