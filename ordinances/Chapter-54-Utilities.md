# CHAPTER 54. UTILITIES

## ARTICLE I. WATER SERVICE

### Sec. 54-1. Water system authority.

The town owns and operates the water distribution system within the corporate limits and as extended.

### Sec. 54-2. Application for service.

Any person desiring water service shall make application to the town. The application shall state the location of the premises to be served and the purpose for which water is to be used.

### Sec. 54-3. Meter installation.

The town shall install a water meter at each service connection. All meters shall remain the property of the town.

### Sec. 54-4. Deposit.

The town may require a deposit from applicants for water service as a condition of establishing service.

### Sec. 54-5. Billing.

Water service shall be billed monthly. Bills shall be rendered to the customer of record at the address of service or such other address as the customer may designate.

### Sec. 54-6. Delinquency.

(a) A customer whose account becomes delinquent shall receive a ten-day notice of delinquency.

(b) Following the ten-day notice, the town may interrupt water service with 24-hour advance notice.

(c) A reconnection fee of $25.00 shall be charged before service is restored.

### Secs. 54-7—54-30. Reserved.

### Sec. 54-31. Water shortage declaration.

The town manager may declare a water shortage when, in his judgment, existing water supplies are or may become insufficient to meet normal demands.

### Sec. 54-32. Mandatory conservation — Stage I.

Upon declaration of a Stage I water shortage, nonessential outdoor water use shall be restricted to alternate days based on address.

### Sec. 54-33. Mandatory conservation — Stage II.

Upon declaration of a Stage II water shortage, outdoor watering shall be prohibited except for hand watering of food gardens and newly planted vegetation.

### Sec. 54-34. Mandatory conservation — Stage III.

Upon declaration of a Stage III water shortage, all nonessential outdoor water use shall be prohibited.

### Sec. 54-35. Mandatory conservation — Stage IV.

Upon declaration of a Stage IV water shortage, all outdoor water use shall be prohibited, and indoor water use shall be restricted to essential health and safety needs only.

### Sec. 54-99. Lifting restrictions.

Water shortage restrictions shall be lifted by the town manager upon determination that sufficient water supplies have been restored to meet normal demands.

**Secs. 54-100—54-126. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE IV. SEWER SERVICE

### DIVISION 1. GENERALLY

### Sec. 54-127. Purpose and policy.

It is the purpose of this article to provide for the regulation of the use of public and private sewers and drains, private wastewater disposal, the installation and connection of building sewers, and the discharge of waters and wastes into the public sewer system, and to provide penalties for violations thereof; in order to prevent the introduction of pollutants into the wastewater system which will interfere with the operation of the system or contaminate the resulting sludge; prevent the introduction of pollutants into the municipal wastewater system which will pass through the system inadequately treated into receiving waters or the atmosphere; improve the opportunity to recycle and reclaim wastewaters and sludge from the system; and provide for equitable distribution of the cost of the municipal wastewater system.

### Sec. 54-128. Definitions.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

*Act* or *the Act* means the Federal Water Pollution Control Act, also known as the Clean Water Act, as amended, 33 U.S.C. 1251 et seq.

*BOD (biochemical oxygen demand)* means the quantity of oxygen utilized in the biochemical oxidation of organic matter under standard laboratory procedures for five days at 20°C.

*Building sewer* means a sewer conveying wastewater from the premises of a user to the public sewer.

*Bypass* means the intentional diversion of waste streams from any portion of a user's treatment facility.

*Categorical pretreatment standards* means national pretreatment standards specifying quantities or concentrations of pollutants or pollutant properties which may be discharged to a POTW by existing or new industrial users in specific industrial subcategories.

*Director* means the director of public works or his authorized representative.

*EPA* means the United States Environmental Protection Agency.

*Grab sample* means a sample which is taken from a waste stream on a one-time basis with no regard to the flow in the waste stream and over a period of time not to exceed 15 minutes.

*Indirect discharge* means the introduction of pollutants into a POTW from any nondomestic source regulated under section 307(b), (c) or (d) of the Act.

*Industrial user* means a source of indirect discharge.

*Interference* means a discharge which, alone or in conjunction with a discharge from other sources, inhibits or disrupts the POTW, its treatment processes or operations, or its sludge processes, use or disposal.

*New source* means any building, structure, facility, or installation from which there is or may be a discharge of pollutants, the construction of which commenced after the publication of proposed pretreatment standards under section 307(c) of the Act.

*Pass through* means a discharge which exits the POTW into waters of the United States in quantities or concentrations which, alone or in conjunction with a discharge from other sources, is a cause of a violation of any requirement of the POTW's NPDES permit.

*Pollutant* means dredged spoil, solid waste, incinerator residue, filter backwash, sewage, garbage, sewage sludge, munitions, medical wastes, chemical wastes, biological materials, radioactive materials, heat, wrecked or discarded equipment, rock, sand, cellar dirt and industrial, municipal, and agricultural waste discharged into water.

*POTW* means a publicly owned treatment works, including any devices and systems used in the storage, treatment, recycling, and reclamation of municipal sewage and industrial wastes of a liquid nature.

*Pretreatment* means the reduction of the amount of pollutants, the elimination of pollutants, or the alteration of the nature of pollutant properties in wastewater prior to or in lieu of discharging or otherwise introducing such pollutants into a POTW.

*Significant industrial user (SIU)* means any industrial user of the POTW that: (1) is subject to categorical pretreatment standards; or (2) discharges an average of 25,000 gallons per day or more of process wastewater; or (3) contributes a process waste stream which makes up five percent or more of the average dry weather hydraulic or organic capacity of the POTW treatment plant; or (4) is designated as such by the director.

*Slug* means any discharge of a non-routine, episodic nature, including but not limited to an accidental spill or a non-customary batch discharge, which has a reasonable potential to cause interference or pass through.

*Upset* means an exceptional incident in which there is unintentional and temporary noncompliance with applicable pretreatment standards because of factors beyond the reasonable control of the user.

*Wastewater* means the spent water of a community. From the standpoint of source, it may be a combination of the liquid and water-carried wastes from residences, commercial buildings, industrial plants and institutions, together with any groundwater, surface water and stormwater that may be present.

### Sec. 54-130. Prohibited discharge standards.

(a) General prohibitions. No user shall introduce or cause to be introduced into the POTW any pollutant or wastewater which causes pass through or interference.

(b) Specific prohibitions. No user shall introduce or cause to be introduced into the POTW the following pollutants, substances, or wastewater:

(1) Pollutants which create a fire or explosion hazard in the POTW, including, but not limited to, waste streams with a closed-cup flashpoint of less than 140 degrees Fahrenheit or 60 degrees Celsius using the test methods specified in 40 CFR 261.21.

(2) Wastewater with a pH less than 5.0 or more than 11.0.

(3) Solid or viscous substances which may cause obstruction to the flow in a sewer or other interference with the operation of the wastewater treatment facilities.

(4) Any wastewater containing pollutants of sufficient quantity to interfere with the POTW.

(5) Any wastewater with objectionable color not removed in the treatment process.

(6) Any wastewater having a temperature greater than 150 degrees Fahrenheit.

(7) Any pollutants, including oxygen demanding pollutants, released in a discharge at a flow rate and/or pollutant concentration which will cause interference with the POTW.

(8) Petroleum oil, nonbiodegradable cutting oil, or products of mineral oil origin in amounts that will cause interference or pass through.

(9) Pollutants which result in the presence of toxic gases, vapors, or fumes within the POTW in a quantity that may cause acute worker health and safety problems.

(10) Any trucked or hauled pollutants, except at discharge points designated by the director.

(11) Noxious or malodorous liquids, gases, solids, or other wastewater which, either singly or by interaction with other wastes, are sufficient to create a public nuisance or a hazard to life.

(12) Wastewater which imparts color which cannot be removed by the treatment process.

(13) Wastewater containing any radioactive wastes or isotopes except in compliance with applicable state or federal regulations.

(14) Stormwater, surface water, groundwater, artesian well water, roof runoff, subsurface drainage, swimming pool drainage, condensate, deionized water, noncontact cooling water, and unpolluted industrial wastewater, unless specifically authorized by the director.

(15) Sludge, screenings, or other residues from the pretreatment of industrial wastes.

(16) Medical wastes, except as specifically authorized by the director.

(17) Wastewater causing, alone or in conjunction with other sources, the treatment plant effluent to fail a toxicity test.

(18) Detergents, surface-active agents, or other substances which might cause excessive foaming in the POTW.

(19) Fats, oils, or greases of animal or vegetable origin in concentrations greater than 400 mg/l.

(20) Any waste or wastewater containing concentrations of heavy metals in excess of applicable limits.

(21) Any hazardous waste defined in RCRA which is not excluded from regulation.

(22) Any waste containing pathogenic organisms in sufficient quantities to affect POTW operations.

(23) Any substance which could cause the POTW to violate its NPDES permit.

(24) Any waste which, by reason of its nature or quantity, requires special collection, handling, or disposal.

The national categorical pretreatment standards found at 40 CFR chapter I, subchapter N, parts 405—471 are hereby incorporated.

### Sec. 54-133. State requirements.

Industrial users must comply with all applicable state pretreatment requirements.

### Sec. 54-134. Right of revision.

The town reserves the right to establish by ordinance more stringent limitations or requirements on discharges to the POTW if deemed necessary.

### Sec. 54-135. Dilution.

No user shall ever increase the use of process water or, in any other way, attempt to dilute a discharge as a partial or complete substitute for adequate treatment.

### Sec. 54-136. Pretreatment of wastewater.

Users shall provide such pretreatment as may be required to comply with this article and applicable pretreatment standards.

### Sec. 54-137. Accidental discharge and slug control plans.

(a) Each significant industrial user shall submit to the director a written slug control plan.

(b) Accidental discharges shall be reported immediately to the director by telephone and followed by a written report within five days.

### Sec. 54-138. Hauled wastewater.

Hauled wastewater may be discharged to the POTW only at locations designated by the director and at such times as the director may determine.

### Sec. 54-139. Use of public sewers.

(a) It shall be unlawful for any person to place, deposit, or permit to be deposited in any unsanitary manner on public or private property any human or animal excrement, garbage, or other objectionable waste.

(b) Where a public sewer is within 200 feet of any building used for human occupancy, employment, recreation, or other purposes and access to the public sewer is reasonably available, the building shall be connected to the public sewer within 60 days of receiving written notice from the director.

### Sec. 54-140. Regulations for building sewers.

Building sewers shall be constructed and maintained in accordance with specifications and standards established by the director.

### Sec. 54-141. Confidential information.

Information and data submitted by an industrial user may be claimed as confidential in accordance with 40 CFR 2.302.

### Sec. 54-142. Enforcement.

(a) Notification of violation. When the director finds that a user has violated any provision of this article, he shall serve written notice of the violation.

(b) Consent orders. The director may enter into consent orders with users agreeing to specific schedules of compliance.

(c) Show cause hearing. The director may order any user who causes or allows an unauthorized discharge to show cause before the director why the proposed enforcement action should not be taken.

(d) Compliance orders. The director may issue an order directing compliance with pretreatment requirements.

(e) Emergency suspension. The director may suspend water and sewer service when necessary to stop an actual or threatened discharge presenting imminent and substantial danger.

(f) Permit termination. Permits may be revoked for cause.

(g) Civil penalties. Any user who violates any provision of this article shall be liable for a civil penalty not to exceed $10,000 per day per violation.

(h) Criminal. Any person who knowingly violates any provision of this article may be prosecuted under applicable state and federal law.

(i) Injunctive relief. The director may seek injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.

(j) Water severance. The town may sever the water connection of any premises discharging in violation of this article.

(k) Public nuisance. Any violation of this article is hereby declared a public nuisance.

### Sec. 54-143. Annual publication of significant noncompliance.

The director shall annually publish in the local newspaper a list of the users which, during the previous 12 months, were in significant noncompliance with applicable pretreatment requirements.

**Secs. 54-144—54-169. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 2. FEES

### Sec. 54-170. Purpose of division.

The purpose of this division is to provide for the equitable distribution of the cost of the operation and maintenance of the POTW and to provide funds for capital improvements.

### Sec. 54-171. User charges.

(a) Each user shall pay a user charge based on the volume of wastewater discharged to the public sewer.

(b) User charges shall be reviewed at least annually and revised to reflect actual costs.

### Sec. 54-172. Surcharges.

Industrial users shall be assessed surcharges for discharges exceeding the following thresholds:

- BOD greater than 250 mg/l
- TSS greater than 250 mg/l
- COD greater than 500 mg/l
- Oil and grease greater than 40 mg/l

Surcharges shall be calculated based on the mass emission rate of pollutants exceeding the threshold concentrations.

### Sec. 54-173. Pretreatment program administration charges.

Charges for pretreatment program administration shall include:

(1) Permit application review and processing.

(2) Permit issuance.

(3) Compliance inspection and sampling.

(4) Compliance monitoring.

(5) Report review and recordkeeping.

**Secs. 54-174—54-199. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 3. WASTEWATER DISCHARGE PERMITS

### Sec. 54-200. Permission to connect required.

No user shall connect to, use, or discharge to the public sewer system without first obtaining written permission from the director.

### Sec. 54-201. SIU permit required.

(a) All significant industrial users proposing to connect to or to discharge to the POTW shall obtain a wastewater discharge permit before connecting to or discharging to the POTW.

(b) Existing significant industrial users shall apply for a wastewater discharge permit within 180 days of the effective date of this article.

(c) Proposed new significant industrial users shall apply at least 90 days prior to connecting to or discharging to the POTW.

### Sec. 54-202. Application.

Users required to obtain a wastewater discharge permit shall complete and file with the director an application in the form prescribed by the director, which shall include at minimum:

(1) Name, address, and location.

(2) SIC number.

(3) Wastewater constituents and characteristics.

(4) Time and duration of discharge.

(5) Average daily and instantaneous peak wastewater flow rates.

(6) Site plans, floor plans, mechanical and plumbing plans, and details to show all sewers, floor drains, and appurtenances by size, location, and elevation.

(7) Description of activities, facilities, and plant processes on the premises.

(8) Where known, the nature and concentration of any pollutants in the discharge which are limited by any town, state, or federal pretreatment standards.

(9) Each product produced by type, amount, process, or processes, and the rate of production.

(10) Type and amount of raw materials processed.

(11) Number and type of employees, and hours of work.

(12) Any other information as may be deemed necessary by the director.

(13) Description of existing pretreatment facilities.

(14) Description of proposed pretreatment facilities.

### Sec. 54-203. Required signature and certification.

All permit applications, user reports, and certifications required by this article shall be signed by an authorized representative.

### Sec. 54-204. Application review.

The director shall acknowledge receipt of an application within 30 days.

### Sec. 54-205. Tentative determination and draft permit.

The director shall prepare a tentative determination and draft permit for each complete application.

### Sec. 54-206. Synopsis and fact sheet.

The director shall prepare a synopsis or fact sheet for each draft permit.

### Sec. 54-207. Final action.

(a) The director shall take final action on a permit application within 90 days of receipt of a complete application.

(b) Final actions include:

(1) Issuance of a permit.

(2) Issuance of a permit with conditions.

(3) Denial with statement of reasons.

(4) Request for additional information.

(5) Transfer of application to another agency.

(6) Referral for enforcement action.

### Sec. 54-208. Hearings.

(a) Any person aggrieved by a final decision may demand a hearing before a hearing officer within 30 days of the decision.

(b) The hearing officer shall hold a hearing within 45 days of demand.

(c) The aggrieved person may appeal the hearing officer's decision to the town council within 10 days.

(d) The town council shall make a final decision within 90 days.

(e) Judicial review shall be sought within 30 days of the council's decision.

### Sec. 54-209. Modification.

(a) The director may modify a permit in the following simplified circumstances:

(1) Correction of typographical or arithmetic errors.

(2) Increases in production within permit limits.

(3) New or modified categorical standards with 9-month compliance schedule.

### Sec. 54-210. Conditions.

Wastewater discharge permits shall contain the following required conditions and may contain the following optional conditions:

(a) *Required conditions:*

(1) Statement of duration.

(2) Statement of non-transferability.

(3) Effluent limits, including local, state, and federal limits.

(4) Self-monitoring, sampling, reporting, notification, and recordkeeping requirements.

(5) Statement of applicable civil and criminal penalties.

(6) Requirements for compliance schedules.

(7) Requirements for submission of technical reports or discharge reports.

(8) Requirements for maintaining and retaining plant records.

(9) Requirements for notification of the director of any new introduction of wastewater constituents.

(10) Requirements for notification of the director of any substantial change in the volume or character of pollutants discharged.

(11) Requirements for notification of slug discharges.

(12) Requirements for notification of bypass.

(13) Requirements for notification of upset.

(14) Requirements for compliance with applicable pretreatment standards.

(15) Requirement for proper operation and maintenance of any pretreatment facility.

(16) Other conditions deemed appropriate by the director.

(17) Requirement to develop and implement a slug control plan.

(b) *Optional conditions:* The director may include any of 16 additional conditions as appropriate to each individual user.

### Sec. 54-211. Duration.

Permits shall be issued for a specified time period not to exceed five years.

### Sec. 54-212. Transfer.

Wastewater discharge permits are issued to a specific user for a specific operation and are not transferable to another user or location.

### Sec. 54-213. Reissuance.

A user with an expiring wastewater discharge permit shall apply for reissuance no later than 180 days prior to expiration.

**Secs. 54-214—54-234. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 4. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

### Sec. 54-235. Baseline monitoring reports.

Within 180 days after the effective date of a categorical pretreatment standard, or 180 days after the final administrative decision on a category determination, existing significant industrial users subject to such categorical pretreatment standards shall submit to the director a baseline monitoring report.

### Sec. 54-236. Compliance schedule progress reports.

Any user subject to a compliance schedule shall submit progress reports to the director no later than 14 days following each date in the schedule.

### Sec. 54-237. Reports on compliance with categorical standards.

Within 90 days following the date for final compliance with applicable categorical pretreatment standards, or following commencement of the introduction of wastewater into the POTW by a new significant industrial user, any user subject to pretreatment standards and requirements shall submit to the director a report indicating the nature and concentration of all pollutants in the discharge from the regulated process.

### Sec. 54-238. Periodic compliance reports.

(a) All significant industrial users shall submit to the director a report at least twice per year, in June and December, indicating the nature and concentration of pollutants in the discharge.

(b) All analyses shall be performed in accordance with procedures established by the EPA.

### Sec. 54-239. Reports of changed conditions.

Each user shall notify the director of any planned significant changes to the user's operations or system which might alter the nature, quality, or volume of its wastewater at least 30 days before the change. Flow increases of 20 percent or more constitute a significant change.

### Sec. 54-240. Reports of potential problems.

(a) In the case of any discharge, including, but not limited to, accidental discharges, discharges of a non-routine nature, or slug discharges, that might cause problems for the POTW, including any discharge that would violate any prohibition of this article, the user shall immediately telephone and notify the director.

(b) Within five days following such discharge, the user shall submit to the director a detailed written report describing the cause of the discharge and the measures to be taken by the user to prevent similar future occurrences.

### Sec. 54-241. Reports from nonpermitted users.

All users not required to obtain a wastewater discharge permit shall provide appropriate reports to the director upon request.

### Sec. 54-242. Notice of violation and repeat sampling.

If sampling by a user indicates a violation, the user shall notify the director within 24 hours of becoming aware of the violation. The user shall also repeat the sampling and analysis and submit the results to the director within 30 days after becoming aware of the violation.

### Sec. 54-243. Notification of hazardous waste discharge.

(a) Users shall notify the director, the EPA regional waste management division director, and the state hazardous waste authorities in writing of any discharge into the POTW of a substance which would be a hazardous waste if disposed of on land.

(b) Notification must occur within 180 days of the discharge.

(c) Industrial users discharging more than 100 kg/month of hazardous waste shall provide additional information.

### Sec. 54-244. Analytical requirements.

All pollutant analyses shall be performed in accordance with the techniques prescribed in 40 CFR Part 136.

### Sec. 54-245. Sample collection.

Samples collected to satisfy reporting requirements shall be based on data obtained through appropriate sampling and analysis performed during the period covered by the report. Flow proportional composite samples shall be used; grab samples may be used for oil and grease, temperature, pH, cyanide, total phenols, sulfides, and volatile organic compounds.

### Sec. 54-246. Timing.

Written reports will be deemed to have been submitted on the date postmarked.

### Sec. 54-247. Recordkeeping.

Users subject to the reporting requirements of this article shall retain, and make available for inspection and copying, all records of information obtained pursuant to any monitoring activities required by this article, and documentation associated with best management practices for a minimum of three years.

**Secs. 54-248—54-272. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 5. COMPLIANCE MONITORING

### Sec. 54-273. Monitoring facilities.

The director may require any industrial user to construct and maintain, at the user's expense, appropriate monitoring facilities to allow inspection, sampling, and flow measurement of the building sewer or internal drainage systems. Such facilities shall be constructed within 90 days of the director's request.

### Sec. 54-274. Inspection and sampling.

The director shall have the right to enter the premises of any user to determine whether the user is complying with all requirements of this article and any wastewater discharge permit issued hereunder. A user who refuses access is in violation of this article.

### Sec. 54-275. Search warrants.

If the user has security measures in force which would require proper identification and clearance before entry into the premises, the user shall make the necessary arrangements to allow access to representatives of the director. In cases where the user refuses to allow the director access to the premises, the director may seek a search warrant.

**Secs. 54-276—54-298. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 6. AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

### Sec. 54-299. Upset.

(a) An upset shall constitute an affirmative defense to an action brought for noncompliance with pretreatment standards if the requirements of this section are met.

(b) A user claiming upset shall notify the director by telephone within 24 hours of becoming aware of the upset condition.

(c) The burden of proof for establishing the defense of upset shall be on the user.

### Sec. 54-300. Prohibited discharge standards defense.

A user shall have an affirmative defense to an enforcement action brought against it for noncompliance with the general prohibitions of section 54-130 if it can prove that it did not know or have reason to know that its discharge would cause pass through or interference.

### Sec. 54-301. Bypass.

(a) Bypass for essential maintenance. A user may allow any bypass to occur which does not cause pretreatment standards or requirements to be violated, but only if it also is for essential maintenance to ensure efficient operation.

(b) Notice.

(1) Anticipated bypass. If a user knows in advance of the need for a bypass, it shall submit prior notice to the director, if possible at least ten days before the date of the bypass.

(2) Unanticipated bypass. A user shall submit oral notice to the director within 24 hours from the time it becomes aware of an unanticipated bypass, followed by a written report within five days.

**Secs. 54-302—54-330. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE V. STORMWATER QUALITY MANAGEMENT AND DISCHARGE CONTROL

### DIVISION 1. TITLE, PURPOSE AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Sec. 54-331. Title.

This article shall be known as the "Stormwater Quality Management and Discharge Control Ordinance."

### Sec. 54-332. Authority.

This article is adopted pursuant to the authority granted to the town under G.S. ch. 160A.

### Sec. 54-333. Purpose.

The purposes of this article are to:

(1) Protect, maintain, and enhance the public health, safety, and general welfare by establishing minimum requirements and procedures to control the adverse effects of increased post-development stormwater runoff and non-point and point source pollution associated with new development and significant redevelopment.

(2) Protect the water resources of the town.

(3) Satisfy NPDES stormwater permit requirements.

### Sec. 54-334. Applicability.

(a) This article applies to all development and redevelopment within the incorporated limits of the town.

(b) Stormwater management shall comply with the town's BMP manual.

### Sec. 54-335. Exceptions.

(a) Development that is existing and permitted prior to the effective date of this article.

(b) Bona fide farm operations as defined by state law.

### Sec. 54-336. Definitions.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:

*Applicant* means a property owner or agent of a property owner who has filed an application for a stormwater management permit.

*Best management practices (BMPs)* means structural or nonstructural measures, practices, techniques or devices employed to avoid or minimize soil, sediment or pollutants carried in runoff to waters of the state.

*Bona fide farm* means a farm that qualifies as a bona fide farm under state law.

*Building* means any structure built for support, shelter, or enclosure for any occupancy or storage.

*Built-upon area* means impervious surface and partially impervious surface to the extent that the partially impervious surface does not allow water infiltration.

*Channel* means a natural or artificial watercourse with a definite bed and banks that conducts continuously or periodically flowing water.

*Channel bank* means the part of the channel cross-section that restricts lateral movement of water at normal flow.

*Town* means the Town of China Grove, North Carolina.

*Clean Water Act* means the Federal Water Pollution Control Act (33 U.S.C. § 1251 et seq.), as hereafter amended.

*Construction activity* means activities subject to NPDES construction permits, including clearing, grading, and excavating that results in land disturbance of greater than or equal to one acre.

*Design storm* means a hypothetical storm defined by a statistical frequency of occurrence and used for designing stormwater management facilities.

*Development* means any land disturbing activity which adds to or changes the amount of impervious or partially impervious cover on a land area, or which otherwise decreases the infiltration of precipitation into the soil.

*Drainage structures* means structures through which stormwater flows, including pipes, culverts, ditches, and channels.

*Grandfathered rights* means rights recognized through the vested rights provisions of this article.

*Hazardous materials* means any substance designated under 40 CFR Parts 116 and 117, petroleum products, and any substance defined as hazardous under applicable state or federal law.

*Illicit connections* means any man-made conveyance connecting an illicit discharge directly to a municipal separate storm sewer.

*Illicit discharge* means any discharge to a municipal separate storm sewer that is not composed entirely of stormwater except as exempted herein.

*Impervious surface* means any surface that prevents or significantly impedes the infiltration of water into the soil.

*Industrial activity* means activities subject to NPDES industrial permits as defined in 40 CFR § 122.26(b)(14).

*Intermittent streams* means streams that flow only in direct response to precipitation in the immediate watershed and whose channel is above the water table. For purposes of this article, an intermittent stream drains a watershed of 300 acres or less.

*Land disturbing activities* means any use of the land by any person in residential, industrial, educational, institutional, or commercial development that results in a change in the natural cover or topography of the land.

*NPDES* means National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System.

*Natural drainage way* means a swale, gully, rill, dry branch, or similar feature formed by erosive action of runoff water.

*Non-stormwater discharge* means any discharge to the storm drain system that is not composed entirely of stormwater.

*Owner* means the legal or beneficial owner of a site.

*Perennial stream* means a stream or the part of a stream that flows continuously during all of the calendar year as a result of groundwater discharge or surface runoff. For purposes of this article, a perennial stream drains a watershed of more than 300 acres.

*Pollutant* means anything which causes or contributes to pollution, including, but not limited to, paints, varnishes, solvents, oil and other automotive fluids, non-hazardous liquid and solid wastes, yard wastes, refuse, rubbish, garbage, litter, or other discarded or abandoned objects, ordinances and accumulations, so that same may cause or contribute to pollution; floatables; pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers; hazardous substances and wastes; sewage, fecal coliform and pathogens; dissolved and particulate metals; animal wastes; wastes and residues that result from constructing a building or structure; and noxious or offensive matter of any kind.

*Pollution* means the man-made or man-induced alteration of the chemical, physical, biological, and radiological integrity of water.

*Premises* means any building, lot, parcel of land, or portion of land whether improved or unimproved.

*Riparian buffer* means a vegetated area near a stream that plays an important role in maintaining the ecological integrity of the stream.

*Sheet flow* means diffuse overland flow that occurs on slopes before stormwater concentrates into channels.

*Soil erosion and sedimentation control plan* means a plan for the control of soil erosion and sedimentation resulting from land-disturbing activities.

*Storm drain system* means the conveyance system for stormwater drainage including pipes, ditches, channels, swales, culverts, and other drainage structures.

*Stormwater* means runoff and drainage from rainfall and snowmelt.

*Stormwater administrator* means the town official designated to administer this article.

*Stormwater facilities* means any facility, including those listed above, designed or used for collecting, conveying, controlling, treating, or discharging stormwater.

*Stormwater BMP manual* means the most recent version of the technical guidance document that provides design standards and specifications for stormwater best management practices adopted by the town.

*Stream buffer* means a natural or planted vegetation strip maintained alongside a stream.

*Structural stormwater facility* means a constructed stormwater BMP designed to remove pollutants from stormwater runoff.

*Structure* means anything constructed or erected, the use of which requires location on the ground, or attachment to something having location on the ground.

*Vegetative buffer* means an area of natural or planted vegetation through which stormwater flows in a diffuse manner so that runoff does not concentrate into a channel.

*Vested rights* means rights that have been established under applicable law to develop property.

*Water dependent structures* means structures which must be located in, on, or adjacent to water to serve their basic purpose.

*Waters of the United States* means waters as defined in 40 CFR Part 122.2.

*Wetland* means an area that is inundated or saturated by surface or ground water at a frequency and duration sufficient to support, and that under normal circumstances does support, a prevalence of vegetation typically adapted for life in saturated soil conditions.

### Sec. 54-337. Interpretation.

This article shall be interpreted and applied so as to achieve its stated purposes.

### Sec. 54-338. Permits.

(a) A stormwater permit is required for all development subject to this article.

(b) The stormwater administrator shall not issue a stormwater permit unless:

(1) The applicant has submitted all required information.

(2) The application demonstrates compliance with all applicable requirements.

(3) The required fees have been paid.

(4) Any required performance securities have been provided.

(c) Permits shall be valid for one year.

### Sec. 54-339. Fees.

The town shall establish a schedule of fees for stormwater permits.

### Sec. 54-340. Applicability and vested rights.

Vested rights shall be established by the following:

(1) A valid building permit issued before the effective date of this article.

(2) A development agreement entered into pursuant to G.S. 160A-400.20 et seq. before the effective date of this article.

(3) A site-specific development plan that has been approved through a formal process before the effective date of this article.

### Sec. 54-341. Responsibility for administration.

The stormwater administrator shall be responsible for administering this article.

### Sec. 54-342. Variances and appeals.

(a) A person aggrieved by a decision of the stormwater administrator may appeal to the appeals officer within 30 days.

(b) The appeals officer shall schedule a hearing within 14 days of receipt of the appeal.

(c) The appeals officer shall issue a ruling within 14 days after the hearing.

(d) The aggrieved person may appeal further to the town manager within 30 days.

(e) The town manager shall hold a public hearing within 30 days of the appeal.

(f) The town manager shall issue a ruling within 30 days after the hearing.

### Sec. 54-343. Severability.

If any provision of this article is for any reason held invalid or unconstitutional, such portion shall be deemed severable.

### Sec. 54-344. Regulatory consistency.

This article shall be construed to assure consistency with the requirements of NPDES permits.

### Sec. 54-345. Ultimate responsibility of discharger.

Nothing in this article shall relieve any person from ultimate legal responsibility for the quality of stormwater discharges from their property.

### Sec. 54-346. Stormwater management.

(a) A stormwater management plan is required when:

(1) The project creates 20,000 square feet or more of impervious surface.

(2) Land disturbance involves stream buffer disturbance.

(3) Fill or excavation exceeds 1,000 cubic feet.

(4) Total land disturbance covers one acre or more.

(b) Design standards:

(1) Stormwater control facilities shall be designed to capture and treat the runoff from the first one inch of rainfall.

(2) Drawdown time shall be 48–120 hours.

(3) Facilities shall achieve 85 percent total suspended solids removal.

(4) Detention shall control the one-year and ten-year storm events.

(5) Where built-upon area density exceeds 24 percent, stormwater management shall comply with 15A NCAC 2H.1008(c).

(c) A recorded easement and inspection/maintenance agreement shall be required for all stormwater facilities.

### Sec. 54-347. Stream buffers.

(a) *Perennial stream buffers.* A stream buffer of 50 feet plus four times the slope percentage from the channel bank shall be maintained along perennial streams, with a maximum buffer width of 120 feet. An additional 20-foot vegetated setback shall be maintained adjacent to the 50-foot buffer.

(b) *Intermittent stream buffers.* A stream buffer of 30 feet shall be maintained along intermittent streams, with the first 20 feet undisturbed.

(c) *Exemptions.* The following activities are exempt from stream buffer requirements:

(1) Water-dependent structures.

(2) Utility crossings approved by the state.

(3) Road crossings approved by the state.

(4) Greenways and recreational trails.

(5) Streambank stabilization projects.

(6) Activities in an existing developed area that were previously approved.

**Secs. 54-348—54-360. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 2. DISCHARGE PROHIBITIONS

### Sec. 54-361. Illegal discharges.

No person shall throw, drain, or otherwise discharge, cause, or allow others under its control to throw, drain, or otherwise discharge into the municipal separate storm sewer system any non-stormwater discharge except as specifically exempted.

### Sec. 54-362. Illicit connections.

(a) All illicit connections shall be removed within one year of the effective date of this article.

(b) Connections for hazardous materials shall be removed immediately.

### Sec. 54-363. Waste disposal prohibitions.

No person shall dispose of, or permit the disposal of, any waste in any manner that will result in the introduction of pollutants into the storm drain system.

### Sec. 54-364. NPDES permit compliance.

Nothing in this article shall be construed to prohibit any discharge that is in compliance with a valid and current NPDES permit.

**Secs. 54-365—54-394. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 3. REGULATIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

### Sec. 54-395. Best management practices.

(a) The town shall establish BMPs for the control of pollutants and stormwater discharges.

(b) The stormwater administrator shall submit an annual report to the town council on the implementation and effectiveness of BMPs.

### Sec. 54-396. Eliminate illegal discharges.

The town shall implement a program to detect and eliminate illicit discharges.

### Sec. 54-397. Eliminate or secure approval for illicit connections.

The town shall implement a program to detect and eliminate or secure approval for illicit connections.

### Sec. 54-398. Watercourse protection.

(a) Every person owning property through which a watercourse passes shall keep and maintain that part of the watercourse within the property free of trash, debris, and other obstacles which would pollute, contaminate, or impede the flow of water through the watercourse.

(b) No person shall remove healthy bank vegetation from any watercourse without prior written permission from the stormwater administrator.

### Sec. 54-399. Requirement to remediate.

Whenever the stormwater administrator finds that a person has violated any prohibition or requirement of this article, the stormwater administrator may order that person to remediate the source of the violation.

### Sec. 54-400. Requirement to monitor and analyze.

The stormwater administrator may require any person responsible for a facility or activity that may contribute to stormwater pollution to undertake monitoring and analysis.

### Sec. 54-401. Notification of spills.

(a) *Hazardous materials.* Any person responsible for a facility or activity that experiences a spill of hazardous materials shall call 911 immediately.

(b) *Non-hazardous materials.* Any person responsible for a facility or activity that experiences a spill of non-hazardous pollutants shall notify the stormwater administrator by 5:00 p.m. of the next business day following the spill.

(c) Written confirmation shall be provided within three business days following the oral notification.

(d) Records of all spills shall be maintained for three years.

**Secs. 54-402—54-425. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 4. INSPECTION AND MONITORING

### Sec. 54-426. Authority to inspect.

The stormwater administrator shall be authorized to inspect any facility, activity, or area for the purpose of enforcing this article.

### Sec. 54-427. Authority to sample and test.

The stormwater administrator shall be authorized to conduct sampling, testing, and analysis to determine compliance with this article.

**Secs. 54-428—54-445. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 5. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

### Sec. 54-446. Enforcement.

(a) Written notice of violation shall be issued to any person found to be in violation of this article.

(b) Misdemeanor. Violation of this article is a misdemeanor.

(c) Civil penalties. Any person who violates this article may be subject to:

(1) Phase II civil penalty up to $27,500 per day.

(2) Criminal penalties for negligent violations: $2,500–$25,000 per day and/or up to one year imprisonment.

(3) Criminal penalties for knowing violations: $5,000–$50,000 per day and/or up to three years imprisonment.

(4) Administrative penalties up to $11,000 per violation, maximum $137,500.

(d) Illegal discharge penalties:

(1) First offense involving five gallons or less of domestic waste: $100 per day.

(2) All other illegal discharges: $1,000 per day.

(3) Repeat violations: double previous penalty, up to $10,000 per day.

### Sec. 54-447. Notice of violation.

A notice of violation shall include:

(1) Name and address of the violator.

(2) Address or location of violation.

(3) Statement of violation.

(4) Description of remediation required.

(5) Schedule for remediation.

(6) Statement of penalties.

### Sec. 54-448. Appeal.

Appeals from enforcement actions shall be made pursuant to section 54-342.

### Sec. 54-449. Abatement.

The stormwater administrator may order abatement of any violation.

### Sec. 54-450. Charging cost and liens.

(a) The town may assess costs of abatement against the property owner within 30 days of abatement.

(b) The property owner has 15 days to protest the assessment.

### Sec. 54-451. Urgency abatement.

When an urgent condition poses an imminent threat to public health, safety, or the environment, the stormwater administrator may abate the condition without prior notice.

### Sec. 54-452. Violations.

Violation of any provision of this article is a misdemeanor.

### Sec. 54-453. Compensatory action.

In lieu of or in addition to other penalties, a person may be ordered to perform compensatory actions including storm drain stenciling, educational workshops, or creek cleanup.

### Sec. 54-454. Public nuisance.

Any condition in violation of this article is hereby declared a public nuisance.

### Sec. 54-455. Acts potentially resulting in Federal Clean Water Act violation.

Notwithstanding other provisions of this article, whenever the stormwater administrator finds a discharge of stormwater that is likely to cause or contribute to a violation of the town's NPDES permit, the stormwater administrator shall take all steps necessary to halt the discharge.
