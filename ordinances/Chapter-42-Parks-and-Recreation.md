# CHAPTER 42. PARKS AND RECREATION

## ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

### Sec. 42-1. Registered sex offenders prohibited from town parks.

(a) *Definitions.* As used in this section:

*Park* means any town-owned or town-operated recreational facility or playground.

*Registered sex offender* means any person required to register under the North Carolina Sex Offender and Public Protection Registration Program, G.S. ch. 14, art. 27A.

(b) It shall be unlawful for any registered sex offender to be present in any town park.

(c) Exceptions: A registered sex offender may be present at a town park if the park is being used as a polling place during an election, or if the registered sex offender is attending an official meeting of a governmental entity.

(d) This section is consistent with the Jessica Lunsford Act as applied to offenses committed on or after November 30, 2008.

(e) A violation of this section is a Class 3 misdemeanor, punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 for each entry into a town park.

(f) Within 30 days of the effective date of this section, the town shall post notification of this ordinance at all town park entrances.

**Secs. 42-2—42-18. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE II. PARK REGULATIONS

### Sec. 42-19. Permit for use.

(a) *Application.* Any person seeking to use a town park for a group activity or event shall submit an application to the town containing the following information:

(1) The name and address of the person or organization sponsoring the activity.

(2) The sponsor of the event.

(3) The day and hours of the proposed activity.

(4) The park or portion of a park to be used.

(5) An estimate of the number of persons expected to attend.

(6) Such other information as the town may reasonably require.

(b) *Standards.* The town shall issue a permit unless the proposed activity:

(1) Would unreasonably interfere with the public's enjoyment of the park, or would pose a threat to the health, safety, or recreational use of the park.

(2) Is reasonably anticipated to incite violence, disorder, or other unlawful activity.

(3) Would require an unusual and burdensome expense to the town.

(4) Cannot be accommodated because the park facilities are otherwise reserved.

(c) The permittee shall comply with all park rules and regulations.

(d) The permittee shall be liable for any damages to park property caused by the permitted activity.

### Sec. 42-20. Hours.

Town parks shall be open from sunrise to midnight, or during such other hours as may be posted at each park.

### Sec. 42-21. Vehicles.

(a) No person shall drive any vehicle in a town park except on established park drives.

(b) No person shall park any vehicle in a town park except in areas designated for parking.

### Sec. 42-22. Destruction of town property.

It shall be unlawful for any person to destroy, deface, or injure any town property in a town park.

### Sec. 42-23. Firearms and weapons.

It shall be unlawful for any person to carry, possess, or discharge any firearm or weapon in any town park, except for authorized town employees performing their official duties, law enforcement officers, and persons possessing a valid concealed carry permit.

### Sec. 42-24. Posting advertisements.

It shall be unlawful for any person to post, display, or distribute any advertisement, notice, or circular in any town park without authorization from the town.

### Sec. 42-25. Selling and peddling.

It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or peddle any goods or services in any town park without written authority from the town.

### Sec. 42-26. Fires.

(a) No person shall build or maintain a fire in a town park except in a constructed fireplace or grill provided for that purpose.

(b) No person shall leave a fire unattended in a town park.

(c) Every person who builds a fire in a town park shall completely extinguish the fire before leaving the area.

### Sec. 42-27. Excavations.

No person shall make any excavation in a town park without written permission from the town.

### Sec. 42-28. Games and sports.

(a) Games and sports shall be played only in areas designated for that purpose.

(b) No person shall play any game or sport near or in the area of playground equipment.

### Sec. 42-29. Animals at large and horseback riding.

(a) No person shall allow any animal to run at large in a town park.

(b) Horseback riding shall be permitted only on designated bridle trails.

### Sec. 42-30. Injuring plant material.

It shall be unlawful for any person to injure, deface, or destroy any plant material in a town park.

### Sec. 42-31. Molesting animals.

It shall be unlawful for any person to molest, kill, or harm any animal in a town park.
