# CHAPTER 10. ANIMALS

## ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

### Sec. 10-1. Definitions.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

**Animal control officer** means any person employed by the town or county to enforce this chapter or other animal regulations.

**At large** means off the premises of the owner and not under the immediate control of the owner or other competent person.

**Harbor** means to keep or maintain, or to allow an animal to remain on one's property.

**Health director** means the director of the county health department or a designated representative.

**Keeper** means any person, other than the owner, who has custody or control of an animal.

**Kitten** means a feline under the age of six months.

**Owner** means any person, firm, corporation, organization, or department possessing, harboring, keeping, having an interest in, or having care, custody, or control of an animal.

**Public nuisance** means any animal which: molests passersby or passing vehicles; attacks other animals; trespasses on school grounds; is repeatedly at large; damages private or public property; barks, whines, or howls in an excessive, continuous, or untimely fashion; or creates unsanitary conditions.

**Pup** means a canine under the age of six months.

**Stray** means any animal that is at large and whose owner is unknown or cannot be found.

**Vicious animal** means any animal that has attacked or bitten a human being or domestic animal without provocation, or has demonstrated a propensity to attack or bite human beings or domestic animals without provocation.
(Code 1993, § 4-1)

### Sec. 10-2. Violation of chapter.

Any violation of the provisions of this chapter shall be a misdemeanor punishable as provided in G.S. 14-4 and as otherwise provided in this chapter.
(Code 1993, § 4-2)

### Sec. 10-3. Territorial applicability.

The provisions of this chapter shall apply to areas within the corporate limits of the town.
(Code 1993, § 4-3)

### Sec. 10-4. Fees.

Fees for the impoundment and quarantine of animals shall be established by the town council.
(Code 1993, § 4-4)

### Sec. 10-5. Interference with animal control officer.

No person shall interfere with, hinder, molest, or abuse the animal control officer in the exercise of his duties.
(Code 1993, § 4-5)

### Sec. 10-6. Town declared a bird sanctuary.

The town is hereby declared to be a bird sanctuary. It shall be unlawful for any person to trap, hunt, shoot, or attempt to shoot, or molest in any manner any wild bird or wildfowl, or to rob bird nests within the town; provided, that if starlings or similar birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities of the town, the health authorities shall meet with representatives of the Audubon Society, Bird Club, Garden Club, or Humane Society, or as many of said organizations as are found to exist in the town, after having given at least three days' actual notice of the time and place of said meeting to the representatives of said organizations. If as a result of said meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate such nuisance, then said birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the health authorities under the supervision of the chief of police of the town.
(Code 1993, § 4-11)

### Sec. 10-7. Permission for keeping certain animals.

(a) No person shall keep, raise, or maintain within the town any chickens, turkeys, geese, ducks, or other domestic fowl, pigeons, or any horses, mules, cattle, swine, sheep, goats, or other livestock without first having obtained a permit from the town manager allowing such keeping, raising, or maintenance.

(b) The town manager shall issue a permit under this section only upon the following conditions:

(1) The applicant must own or control a parcel of land of not less than one acre in area.

(2) The animal(s) shall be confined to the property at all times.

(3) The animal shelter and the area in which the animal(s) are kept shall be maintained in a clean and sanitary condition.

(4) A minimum distance of 200 feet from any dwelling other than that of the applicant shall be maintained between the animal shelter and the nearest point of any such dwelling.

(5) All feed shall be stored in rodent-proof containers.

(c) The permit shall be valid for one year and may be renewed annually. The town manager may revoke the permit at any time for cause after notice to the permit holder and an opportunity to be heard.
(Code 1993, § 4-13)

### Sec. 10-8. Livestock near residences.

No person shall keep livestock within 200 feet of any residence other than that of the owner or keeper of such livestock.
(Code 1993, § 4-14)

### Sec. 10-9. Rabies vaccination.

The owner of every dog and cat over four months of age kept within the town shall have such animal vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian at intervals required by state law. Proof of current vaccination shall be a valid certificate or tag issued by the vaccinating veterinarian.
(Code 1993, § 4-15)

### Sec. 10-10. Rabies reporting.

Any person having knowledge of an animal having rabies or suspected of having rabies shall report the same to the animal control officer or health director.
(Code 1993, § 4-16)

### Sec. 10-11. Animal bites.

(a) Any animal that has bitten a person shall be quarantined for a period of ten days from the date of the bite.

(b) The animal shall be quarantined at the owner's expense in a facility approved by the animal control officer or health director.

(c) During the quarantine period, the animal shall be observed for signs of rabies.

(d) If the animal dies during the quarantine period, the animal control officer shall have the animal's head submitted for laboratory examination.

(e) At the expiration of the quarantine period, if the animal appears healthy, it may be released to the owner.

(f) The owner of the biting animal shall immediately notify the animal control officer of the bite.
(Code 1993, § 4-17)

### Sec. 10-12. Impoundment.

(a) The animal control officer is authorized to impound the following animals:

(1) Any animal found at large.

(2) Any animal that is a public nuisance.

(3) Any vicious animal.

(4) Any stray animal.

(5) Any animal required to be quarantined.

(b) An impounded animal may be redeemed by the owner upon payment of the impoundment fee established by the town council and proof of current rabies vaccination.

(c) An impounded animal not redeemed within the time prescribed by the animal control officer may be placed for adoption or humanely destroyed.

(d) Impounded animals shall be retained for a minimum period as established by state law before disposition.
(Code 1993, § 4-18)

### Sec. 10-13. Animals at large.

It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any animal to allow such animal to run at large within the town. Any animal found at large may be impounded by the animal control officer.
(Code 1993, § 4-19)

### Sec. 10-14. Vicious animals.

(a) No owner shall keep a vicious animal within the town unless such animal is securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen or structure that prevents the entry of children and prevents the animal from escaping.

(b) The animal control officer may order the owner of a vicious animal to permanently remove the animal from the town or to have the animal humanely destroyed.
(Code 1993, § 4-20)

### Sec. 10-15. Nuisance animals.

(a) No owner shall keep an animal that constitutes a public nuisance within the town.

(b) The animal control officer may order the owner to abate any nuisance condition.
(Code 1993, § 4-21)

### Sec. 10-16. Right to hearing.

Any person aggrieved by an order of the animal control officer or health director under this chapter shall have the right to a hearing before the town council upon written request filed within ten days of receipt of the order.
(Code 1993, § 4-22)

### Sec. 10-17. Authority to destroy.

The animal control officer is authorized to destroy any animal that is seriously injured and in pain or that is diseased beyond recovery, where immediate destruction is necessary to prevent further suffering or where the owner cannot be located.
(Code 1993, § 4-23)

### Sec. 10-18. Injured or sick animals.

The animal control officer may provide emergency care for injured or sick animals and may require the owner to reimburse the town for the cost of such care.
(Code 1993, § 4-24)

### Sec. 10-19. Unwanted animals.

Any person wishing to surrender an unwanted animal may do so at the animal shelter, subject to such conditions as may be established by the animal control officer.
(Code 1993, § 4-25)

### Sec. 10-20. Cruelty to animals; maintenance of animal pens.

(a) No person shall knowingly and willfully administer poison to any animal or expose any poisonous substance with intent that it be taken by any animal, unless such action is authorized by law.

(b) All pens, kennels, hutches, and other enclosures for animals shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition, free from offensive odors. Any such enclosure that becomes a nuisance to neighboring property owners shall be subject to abatement.
(Code 1993, § 4-26)

### Sec. 10-21. Unlawful removal of vaccination tag.

No person shall remove a vaccination tag from an animal's collar without the permission of the owner.
(Code 1993, § 4-27)

### Sec. 10-22. Animals at town-sponsored events.

(a) **Definitions.** The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection:

**Animal** means any living creature, domestic or exotic, except fish.

**Assistance animal** means any guide dog, signal dog, or other animal individually trained to provide assistance to an individual with a disability.

**Law enforcement agency animal** means an animal owned or used by a law enforcement agency.

(b) No animal shall be permitted at any town-sponsored event held on town property or in any town facility, except:

(1) Assistance animals accompanying persons with disabilities.

(2) Law enforcement agency animals when on official duty.

(3) Animals that are the subject of, or participants in, the event itself, with prior written approval of the town manager.
(Code 1993, § 4-28; Ord. of 5-6-2014)

### Sec. 10-23. Urban archery deer hunting.

Urban archery deer hunting within the corporate limits of the town shall be subject to the following rules:

(1) All applicable state laws and regulations concerning deer hunting shall be complied with.

(2) A valid North Carolina hunting license shall be required.

(3) Only archery equipment shall be used.

(4) Hunting shall be permitted only on private property with the written permission of the property owner.

(5) Hunting shall not be permitted on town-owned property or within any public right-of-way.

(6) Hunters shall use an elevated platform with a minimum height of ten feet above the ground.

(7) Hunting shall be permitted only on parcels of land of at least one acre in size.

(8) Violations of this section shall be subject to the penalty provided in section 1-6.
(Ord. of 10-1-2013)

### Sec. 10-24. Animal waste.

(a) It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any animal to fail to remove feces deposited by the animal on any public street, sidewalk, public park, or other public area, or on any private property without the permission of the property owner.

(b) The owner or keeper shall be responsible for immediate removal and sanitary disposal of such feces.
(Ord. of 5-6-2014)
