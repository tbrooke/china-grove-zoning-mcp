# CHAPTER 14. BUILDINGS AND BUILDING REGULATIONS

## ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

### Sec. 14-1. County inspections department agreement.

The town has entered into an agreement with the county for the provision of building inspection services. The county inspections department shall perform all inspections and issue all permits required by the North Carolina State Building Code within the corporate limits of the town.
(Code 1993, § 6-1)

**Secs. 14-2—14-20. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE II. MINIMUM HOUSING STANDARDS

### Sec. 14-21. Findings; purpose.

Pursuant to G.S. 160A-411, the town finds that there exist within the corporate limits of the town dwellings which are unfit for human habitation due to dilapidation, defects increasing the hazards of fire, accidents, or other calamities, lack of ventilation, light, or sanitary facilities, or due to other conditions rendering such dwellings unsafe or unsanitary, or dangerous or detrimental to the health, safety, or morals, or otherwise inimical to the welfare of the residents. The town further finds that the repair, closing, or demolition of such dwellings is in the interest of the public health, safety, and welfare.
(Code 1993, § 6-26)

### Sec. 14-22. Definitions.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

**Basement** means a portion of a building located partly underground, having direct access to light and air from windows located above the level of the adjoining ground.

**Cellar** means a portion of a building located partly or wholly underground, having an inadequate access to light and air from windows located at or below the level of the adjoining ground.

**Deteriorated** means a dwelling which is in a state of disrepair but which may be brought into conformance with the requirements of this article through reasonable repairs.

**Dilapidated** means a dwelling which is so deteriorated that it cannot be brought into conformance with the requirements of this article through repairs, or one which has deteriorated to the extent that it no longer provides safe and adequate shelter.

**Dwelling** means any building or structure, or part thereof, used and occupied for human habitation or intended to be so used.

**Dwelling unit** means any room or group of rooms located within a dwelling and forming a single habitable unit with facilities which are used, or intended to be used, for living, sleeping, cooking, and eating.

**Extermination** means the control and elimination of insects, rodents, and other pests by eliminating their harborage places; by removing or making inaccessible materials that serve as their food or water; by other approved pest elimination methods.

**Garbage** means the animal and vegetable waste resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking, and consumption of food.

**Habitable room** means a room used for living, sleeping, eating, or cooking but not a bathroom, toilet room, closet, hallway, storage space, or similar space.

**Infestation** means the presence, within or contiguous to, a structure or premises of insects, rodents, vermin, or other pests.

**Multiple dwelling** means any dwelling containing more than two dwelling units.

**Occupant** means any person living, sleeping, cooking, or eating in, or having actual possession of, a dwelling unit.

**Operator** means any person who has charge, care, or control of a structure or premises which is let or offered for occupancy.

**Owner** means any person who, alone or jointly or severally with others, shall have legal title to any dwelling or dwelling unit, with or without accompanying actual possession thereof.

**Parties in interest** means all persons who have interests of record in a dwelling, including owners, mortgagees, and lienholders.

**Plumbing** means and shall include all of the following supplied facilities and equipment: gas pipes; gas-burning equipment; water pipes; garbage disposal units; waste pipes; water closets; sinks; installed dishwashers; lavatories; bathtubs; shower baths; installed clothes washing machines; catch basins; drains; vents; and any other similar supplied fixtures, together with all connections to water, sewer, or gas lines.

**Public authority** means any housing authority or any officer who is in charge of any department or branch of the government of the city, county, or state relating to health, fire, building regulations, or other activities concerning dwellings in the city.

**Rooming unit** means any room or group of rooms forming a single habitable unit used or intended to be used for living and sleeping, but not for cooking or eating purposes.

**Roominghouse** means any dwelling, or that part of any dwelling, containing one or more rooming units in which space is let by the owner or operator to three or more persons.

**Rubbish** means combustible and noncombustible waste materials, except garbage; and the term shall include the residue from the burning of wood, coal, coke, and other combustible materials, paper, rags, cartons, boxes, wood, excelsior, rubber, leather, tree branches, yard trimmings, tin cans, metals, mineral matter, glass, crockery, and dust and other similar materials.

**Supplied** means paid for, furnished, or provided by or under the control of the owner or operator.

**Temporary housing** means any tent, trailer, or other structure used for human shelter which is designed to be transportable and which is not attached to the ground, to another structure, or to any utility system on the same premises for more than 30 consecutive days.

**Unfit for human habitation** means a condition of a dwelling that makes it hazardous to the health and safety of its occupants or the public.
(Code 1993, § 6-27)

### Sec. 14-23. Compliance.

No person shall occupy as owner-occupant, or let to another for occupancy, any dwelling or dwelling unit for the purpose of living, sleeping, cooking, or eating therein which does not comply with the requirements of this article.
(Code 1993, § 6-28)

### Sec. 14-24. Conflicting provisions.

In any case where a provision of this article is found to be in conflict with a provision of any zoning, building, fire, safety, or health ordinance or code of the town existing on the effective date of the ordinance from which this article derives, the provision which establishes the higher standard for the promotion and protection of the health and safety of the people shall prevail.
(Code 1993, § 6-29)

### Sec. 14-25. Violation; penalty.

Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in G.S. 14-4.
(Code 1993, § 6-30)

### Sec. 14-26. Housing inspector.

The code enforcement officer shall be the housing inspector for the purposes of this article. The housing inspector shall have the following powers and duties:

(1) To investigate the dwelling conditions in the town in order to determine which dwellings therein are unfit for human habitation.

(2) To administer oaths and affirmations, examine witnesses, and receive evidence.

(3) To enter upon premises for the purpose of making examinations, provided that such entries shall be made in such manner as to cause the least possible inconvenience to the persons in possession.

(4) To appoint and fix the duties of such officers, agents, and employees as he deems necessary to carry out the purposes of this article.

(5) To delegate any of his functions and powers under this article to such officers and agents as he may designate.

(6) To issue and serve such complaints, orders, and other papers as may be necessary.

(7) To perform such other duties as may be necessary to carry out the purposes of this article.
(Code 1993, § 6-31)

### Sec. 14-27. Appeals board.

The zoning board of adjustment shall serve as the appeals board for this article. The board shall consist of five members appointed for staggered three-year terms by the town council.
(Code 1993, § 6-32)

### Sec. 14-28. Inspections; right of entry.

The housing inspector is authorized to make inspections to determine the condition of dwellings, dwelling units, rooming units, and premises located within the town. The housing inspector is authorized to enter any dwelling, dwelling unit, rooming unit, or premises at any reasonable time for the purpose of performing the duties imposed by this article.
(Code 1993, § 6-33)

### Sec. 14-29. Enforcement and appeal procedure.

(a) Whenever a petition is filed with the housing inspector by a public authority or by at least five residents of the town charging that any dwelling is unfit for human habitation, or whenever it appears to the housing inspector that any dwelling is unfit for human habitation, the housing inspector shall, if his preliminary investigation discloses a basis for such charges, issue and cause to be served upon the owner of and parties in interest in such dwelling a complaint stating the charges and containing a notice that a hearing will be held.

(b) **Order for deteriorated dwelling.** If, after such notice and hearing, the housing inspector determines that the dwelling is deteriorated, he shall state in writing his findings of fact in support of such determination and shall issue and cause to be served upon the owner an order:

(1) Requiring the owner, within the time specified in the order, to repair, alter, or improve such dwelling to bring it into compliance with the requirements of this article; or

(2) Requiring the owner, within the time specified in the order, to vacate and close such dwelling if the owner elects not to repair.

(c) **Order for dilapidated dwelling.** If, after such notice and hearing, the housing inspector determines that the dwelling is dilapidated, he shall state in writing his findings of fact in support of such determination and shall issue and cause to be served upon the owner an order requiring the owner, within the time specified in the order, to remove or demolish such dwelling.

(d) **In personam and in rem remedies.** The remedies provided in this article may be exercised against the owner personally (in personam) or against the property itself (in rem).

(e) **Appeals.** Any person aggrieved by an order of the housing inspector may appeal to the zoning board of adjustment within ten days of the service of the order.

(f) **Petition to superior court.** Any party aggrieved by a final decision of the zoning board of adjustment may petition the superior court for review within 30 days of the board's decision.
(Code 1993, § 6-34)

### Sec. 14-30. Service of complaints and orders.

Complaints or orders issued by the housing inspector pursuant to this article shall be served upon persons either personally or by registered or certified mail.
(Code 1993, § 6-35)

### Sec. 14-31. Repair or demolition authority; placarding.

(a) Upon the failure of the owner to comply with an order of the housing inspector within the time specified therein, the housing inspector is authorized to proceed to cause such dwelling to be repaired, altered, improved, vacated, closed, removed, or demolished, as the case may be.

(b) The housing inspector is authorized to placard any dwelling declared unfit for human habitation as a result of a determination made pursuant to this article. No person shall remove any such placard without the authorization of the housing inspector.
(Code 1993, § 6-36)

### Sec. 14-32. Lien for costs.

The cost of repairs, alterations, improvements, vacating, closing, removal, or demolition of a dwelling by the town pursuant to this article shall be a lien upon the real property upon which such cost was incurred, ranking on a parity with liens of state, county, and municipal taxes.
(Code 1993, § 6-37)

### Sec. 14-33. Alternate remedies.

Nothing in this article shall be construed to abridge or alter the rights of action or remedies which exist at law or in equity. In addition to the remedies provided in this article, the town may bring an action in the general court of justice for injunctive relief or such other relief as may be appropriate.
(Code 1993, § 6-38)

### Sec. 14-34. Structural condition standards.

A dwelling or dwelling unit shall comply with the following requirements:

(1) The foundation, floors, and walls shall be reasonably weathertight, watertight, and rodent-proof; shall be capable of affording privacy; and shall be kept in good repair.

(2) The roof shall be tight and have no defects which admit rain; and roof drainage shall be adequate to prevent dampness in the walls and interior of the structure.

(3) The floors, interior walls, and ceilings shall be capable of affording privacy and of keeping the occupants protected from the elements.

(4) The windows, exterior doors, and basement hatchways shall be reasonably weathertight, watertight, and rodent-proof, and shall be kept in sound working condition and good repair.

(5) The stairs, porches, and appurtenances thereto shall be so constructed that they will not fall or collapse.

(6) The chimneys and all heating and cooking equipment shall be properly installed, shall be maintained in safe and good working condition, and shall be capable of performing the function for which designed.

(7) The plumbing facilities shall be properly installed, shall be maintained in safe and good working condition, and shall be kept free from obstructions, leaks, and defects.

(8) There shall be an adequate hot water supply.

(9) The electrical system shall be properly installed, maintained in safe and good working condition, and shall be kept free from defects.
(Code 1993, § 6-39)

### Sec. 14-35. Plumbing, heating, and electrical standards.

(a) **Plumbing system.** Every dwelling unit shall contain a kitchen sink in good working condition and properly connected to an approved water and sewer system. Every dwelling unit shall contain a room which affords privacy to a person within said room, and which is equipped with a flush water closet and a lavatory basin in good working condition and properly connected to an approved water and sewer system. Every dwelling unit shall contain a tub or shower in good working condition and properly connected to an approved water and sewer system.

(b) **Heating system.** Every dwelling shall have heating facilities which are properly installed and maintained in safe and good working condition and are capable of safely and adequately heating all habitable rooms.

(c) **Electrical system.** Every dwelling shall be wired for electric lights and convenience outlets. Every habitable room shall contain at least two floor or wall-type electric convenience outlets, or one such convenience outlet and one supplied electric light fixture. Every toilet room, bathroom, laundry room, furnace room, and public hall shall contain at least one supplied electric light fixture.
(Code 1993, § 6-40)

### Sec. 14-36. Windows and ventilation.

Every habitable room shall have at least one window or skylight facing directly to the outdoors. The minimum total window area for every habitable room shall be ten percent of the floor area of such room. Whenever walls or other portions of structures face a window of any such room and such light-obstructing structures are located less than three feet from the window and extend to a level above that of the ceiling of the room, such a window shall not be deemed to face directly to the outdoors and shall not be included as contributing to the required minimum total window area. Every habitable room shall have at least one window or skylight which can easily be opened, or such other device as will adequately ventilate the room.
(Code 1993, § 6-41)

### Sec. 14-37. Size and use of rooms.

(a) Every dwelling unit shall contain at least 150 square feet of floor space for the first occupant, at least 100 additional square feet of floor space for each of the next three occupants, and at least 75 additional square feet of floor space for each additional occupant.

(b) In every dwelling unit, every room occupied for sleeping purposes by one occupant shall contain at least 70 square feet of floor area; every such room occupied by two occupants shall contain at least 50 square feet per occupant; and every such room occupied by more than two occupants shall contain at least 35 square feet per occupant.

(c) No cellar space shall be used as a habitable room or dwelling unit.

(d) No basement space shall be used as a habitable room or dwelling unit unless: the floor and walls are impervious to leakage of underground and surface runoff water; the total window area in each room is equal to at least the minimum window area as specified in section 14-36; the total openable window area in each room is equal to at least 45 percent of the minimum window area; and adequate heating facilities are provided.
(Code 1993, § 6-42)

### Sec. 14-38. General maintenance standards.

(a) **Exterior.** The exterior of every dwelling shall be maintained in good repair and shall be kept free from defects. The foundation walls shall be maintained in good repair and shall be kept free from open cracks and breaks and shall be kept in such condition as to prevent the entry of rodents. Every exterior wall shall be free from holes, breaks, loose or rotting boards or timbers, and any other condition which might admit rain or dampness to the interior of the dwelling. Every roof shall be kept tight and have no defects which admit rain. Roof drainage shall be adequate to prevent dampness in the walls and interior of the structure.

(b) **Interior.** Every floor, interior wall, and ceiling shall be kept in sound condition and good repair. Every floor shall be free from loose, warped, protruding, or rotted floorboards. Every interior wall and ceiling shall be maintained in good repair, clean, and free from dampness. Every window, door, and frame shall be kept in sound condition, good repair, and weather-tight.

(c) **Stairs and porches.** Every stair, porch, and appurtenance thereto shall be so constructed and maintained as to be safe to use and capable of supporting the load that normal use may cause to be placed thereon. Every handrail and balustrade shall be firmly fastened and maintained in good condition.

(d) **Bathroom floors.** Every bathroom floor surface and water closet compartment floor surface shall be constructed and maintained so as to be reasonably impervious to water and to permit such floor to be easily kept in a clean and sanitary condition.

(e) **Supplied facilities.** Every supplied facility, piece of equipment, or utility shall be so constructed or installed that it will function safely and effectively.

(f) **Drainage.** Every dwelling shall have adequate drainage.

(g) **Egress.** Every dwelling unit shall have at least two means of egress leading to safe and open space at ground level.
(Code 1993, § 6-43)

### Sec. 14-39. Control of insects, rodents, and garbage.

(a) Every dwelling shall be supplied with screens of not less than 16 mesh per inch for every door and window and other opening to the outside used or intended to be used for ventilation.

(b) Every owner of a dwelling containing two or more dwelling units shall be responsible for the extermination of insects, rodents, vermin, or other pests whenever infestation exists in two or more of the dwelling units or in the shared or public parts of the structure.

(c) Every occupant of a dwelling unit shall be responsible for the extermination of insects, rodents, vermin, or other pests whenever the infestation is limited to his individual dwelling unit.

(d) Every occupant of a dwelling unit shall store and dispose of all rubbish in a clean and sanitary manner.

(e) Every occupant of a dwelling unit shall store and dispose of all garbage in a clean and sanitary manner by placing such garbage in an approved garbage disposal facility or approved leak-proof, covered, outside garbage containers.
(Code 1993, § 6-44)

### Sec. 14-40. Roominghouses.

(a) The requirements of this article relating to dwellings shall apply to roominghouses and rooming units.

(b) At least one water closet, lavatory basin, and bathtub or shower, properly connected to an approved water and sewer system and in good working condition, shall be supplied for each eight persons or fraction thereof residing within a roominghouse, including members of the operator's family whenever they share the use of such facilities.

(c) Every rooming unit shall contain at least 70 square feet of floor space for a single occupant and an additional 50 square feet for each additional occupant.

(d) The operator of every roominghouse shall maintain the roominghouse in a clean and sanitary condition.

(e) Sanitary facilities shall be located on the floor of the roominghouse that they are intended to serve, or on adjacent floors.
(Code 1993, § 6-45)

### Sec. 14-41. Responsibilities of owner and occupants.

(a) Every owner of a dwelling containing two or more dwelling units shall maintain in a clean and sanitary condition the shared or public areas of the dwelling and premises thereof.

(b) Every occupant of a dwelling or dwelling unit shall maintain in a clean and sanitary condition that part of the dwelling, dwelling unit, and premises thereof which he occupies and controls.

(c) Every occupant of a dwelling unit shall dispose of all his rubbish, garbage, and other organic waste in a clean and sanitary manner.

(d) Every occupant shall keep all plumbing fixtures in the dwelling unit used by him clean and sanitary and shall be responsible for the exercise of reasonable care in the proper use and operation of such fixtures.

(e) No occupant shall willfully destroy, deface, or impair any facilities or equipment furnished by the owner.
(Code 1993, § 6-46)

**Secs. 14-42—14-80. Reserved.**

## ARTICLE III. NONRESIDENTIAL BUILDING MAINTENANCE STANDARDS

### DIVISION 1. GENERALLY

#### Sec. 14-81. Purpose.

The purpose of this article is to protect the public health, safety, and welfare by establishing minimum maintenance standards for nonresidential buildings within the corporate limits of the town.
(Code 1993, § 6-56)

#### Sec. 14-82. Findings; authority.

The town council finds that there exist within the corporate limits nonresidential buildings which are deteriorated, dilapidated, unsafe, unsanitary, or otherwise unfit and that such conditions are injurious to the health, safety, and welfare of the residents of the town. This article is enacted pursuant to the authority granted by G.S. 160A-174.
(Code 1993, § 6-57)

#### Sec. 14-83. Scope; jurisdiction.

The provisions of this article shall apply to all nonresidential buildings and structures located within the corporate limits of the town.
(Code 1993, § 6-58)

#### Sec. 14-84. General definitions and interpretations.

(a) Unless the context specifically indicates otherwise, the meaning of terms used in this article shall be as follows:

(1) Words used in the present tense include the future tense.

(2) Words used in the singular include the plural, and words in the plural include the singular.

(3) The word "shall" is mandatory; the word "may" is permissive.

(4) The word "person" includes a firm, association, organization, partnership, trust, company, or corporation, as well as an individual.

(5) The word "used" or "occupied" shall be construed to include the words "intended, designed, or arranged to be used or occupied."

(6) The word "building" or "structure" shall be construed as if followed by the words "or part thereof."

(7) The word "town" means the Town of China Grove, North Carolina.

(8) The word "council" means the town council of China Grove.

(9) The word "inspector" means the nonresidential building maintenance standards inspector.
(Code 1993, § 6-59)

#### Sec. 14-85. Special definitions.

The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

**Building** means any structure, either temporary or permanent, having a roof and used or built for the shelter or enclosure of persons, animals, chattels, or property of any kind.

**Building accessory** means a subordinate building on the same lot as the principal building and used for a purpose customarily incidental to the use of the principal building.

**Building principal** means the primary building on a lot.

**Building code** means the North Carolina State Building Code as adopted and amended.

**Deterioration** means a lowering in quality due to use or neglect.

**Fire hazard** means any thing or act which increases or may cause an increase in the probability of the occurrence of fire, or which may obstruct, delay, hinder, or interfere with the operations of the fire department or the egress of occupants in case of fire.

**Garbage** means the animal and vegetable waste resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking, and consumption of food.

**Infestation** means the presence within or contiguous to a structure or premises of insects, rodents, vermin, or other pests.

**Nonresidential building maintenance standards inspector** means the person designated by the town manager as the inspector for the administration of this article.

**Nuisance** means:

(1) Physical conditions dangerous to human life or detrimental to the health of persons on or near the premises where the conditions exist.

(2) Unsanitary conditions or anything offensive to the senses or dangerous to health, in the opinion of a majority of those competent to judge, in the neighborhood.

(3) Whatever renders air, food, or drink unwholesome or detrimental to the health of human beings.

(4) Any building or part thereof which is structurally unsafe or not provided with adequate egress; or which constitutes a fire hazard, or is otherwise dangerous to human life; or which, in relation to existing use, constitutes a hazard to safety or health.

(5) Any vacant building unsecured against unauthorized entry.

**Occupant** means any person who occupies a building or any part thereof as a tenant, lessee, or for any other purpose.

**Owner** means the owner of record as shown in the records of the register of deeds, or the agent or representative of such owner who has the care, custody, or management of a building.

**Physical valuation** means the estimated cost to replace the existing building with a new building of similar type and quality as of the date of evaluation.

**Plumbing** means all of the following supplied facilities and equipment: gas pipes; gas-burning equipment; water pipes; garbage disposal units; waste pipes; water closets; sinks; lavatories; bathtubs; shower baths; installed clothes washing machines; catch basins; drains; vents; and any other similar supplied fixtures, together with all connections to water, sewer, or gas lines.

**Premises** means a lot, plot, or parcel of land, including any building or structures thereon.

**Public sanitary sewer** means a sewer system owned and maintained by a governmental body.

**Rubbish** means combustible and noncombustible waste materials except garbage.

**Sewage** means human excrement and greywater (household showers, dishwashing operations, etc.).

**Structurally sound** means capable of sustaining the loads imposed on a building or structural element without exceeding the allowable unit stresses for the materials of construction.

**Structure** means anything constructed or erected which requires location on the ground or which is attached to something having a location on the ground.

**Supplied** means paid for, furnished, or provided by or under the control of the owner or operator.
(Code 1993, § 6-60)

#### Sec. 14-86. Duties of owner.

Every owner of a nonresidential building shall:

(1) Maintain the building in compliance with the requirements of this article.

(2) Keep the building free from conditions which constitute a fire hazard or a nuisance.

(3) Maintain all facilities supplied by the owner in a safe and sanitary condition.

(4) Maintain all exterior surfaces in good repair.
(Code 1993, § 6-61)

#### Sec. 14-87. Duties of occupant.

Every occupant of a nonresidential building shall:

(1) Maintain in a clean and sanitary condition all portions of the building and premises which he occupies or controls.

(2) Dispose of garbage and rubbish in a clean and sanitary manner.

(3) Keep all plumbing fixtures clean and sanitary and use them properly.

(4) Not willfully destroy, deface, or impair any facilities or equipment.

(5) Comply with all requirements of this article applicable to occupants.

(6) Not permit conditions to exist on the premises which constitute a public nuisance or fire hazard.
(Code 1993, § 6-62)

#### Sec. 14-88. Relationship to occupancy.

The provisions of this article shall be applicable to nonresidential buildings regardless of when such buildings were erected.
(Code 1993, § 6-63)

#### Sec. 14-89. Conflict with other provisions.

In any case where a provision of this article is found to be in conflict with a provision of any other ordinance or code of the town, the provision which establishes the higher standard for the promotion and protection of the health and safety of the people shall prevail.
(Code 1993, § 6-64)

#### Sec. 14-90. Amendments.

The town council may from time to time amend, supplement, change, modify, or repeal any provision of this article.
(Code 1993, § 6-65)

**Secs. 14-91—14-120. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 2. STANDARDS

#### Sec. 14-121. Unsafe buildings.

(a) A building or structure is especially dangerous to life for the purposes of this article if any of the following conditions exist:

(1) Whenever any door, aisle, passageway, stairway, or other means of exit is not of sufficient width or size, or is not so arranged as to provide safe and adequate means of exit in case of fire or panic.

(2) Whenever the walking surface of any aisle, passageway, stairway, or other means of exit is so warped, worn, loose, torn, or otherwise unsafe as to not provide safe and adequate means of exit in case of fire or panic.

(3) Whenever the stress in any materials, member, or portion thereof, due to all dead and live loads, is more than 1.5 times the working stress or stresses allowed in the building code for new buildings of similar structure, purpose, or location.

(4) Whenever any portion has been damaged by fire, earthquake, wind, flood, or by any other cause, to such an extent that the structural strength or stability thereof is materially less than it was before such catastrophe and is less than the minimum requirements of the building code for new buildings of similar structure, purpose, or location.

(5) Whenever any portion or member or appurtenance thereof is likely to fail, or to become detached or dislodged, or to collapse and thereby injure persons or damage property.

(6) Whenever any portion of a building, or any member, appurtenance, or ornamentation on the exterior thereof is not of sufficient strength or stability, or is not so anchored, attached, or fastened in place so as to be capable of resisting a wind pressure of one-half of that specified in the building code for new buildings of similar structure, purpose, or location.

(7) Whenever any portion thereof has wracked, warped, buckled, or settled to such an extent that walls or other structural portions have materially less resistance to winds or earthquakes than is required in the case of similar new construction.

(8) Whenever the building, or any portion thereof, because of dilapidation, deterioration, or decay; faulty construction; the removal, movement, or instability of any portion of the ground necessary for the purpose of supporting such building; the deterioration, decay, or inadequacy of its foundation; or any other cause is likely to partially or completely collapse.

(9) Whenever, for any reason, the building or structure, or any portion thereof, is manifestly unsafe for the purpose for which it is being used.

(10) Whenever the exterior walls or other vertical structural members list, lean, or buckle to such an extent that a plumb line passing through the center of gravity does not fall inside the middle one-third of the base.

(11) Whenever the building or structure, exclusive of the foundation, shows 33 percent or more damage or deterioration of its supporting member or members, or 50 percent damage or deterioration of its nonsupporting enclosing or outside walls or covering.

(12) Whenever the building or structure has been so damaged by fire, wind, earthquake, or flood, or has become so dilapidated or deteriorated as to become an attractive nuisance to children, harbor vagrants or criminals, or enable persons to resort thereto for the purpose of committing unlawful or immoral acts.

(b) **Enumerated standards.** The following enumerated standards shall apply:

(1) **General.** All buildings or structures shall:

a. Be structurally sound and in good repair.

b. Be free from conditions which constitute a fire hazard.

c. Be free from conditions which constitute a public nuisance.

d. Be maintained so that the building and its equipment are in a safe and sanitary condition.

e. Have its premises and exterior property maintained free from weeds, overgrown brush, dead vegetation, trash, garbage, rubbish, debris, or any condition which constitutes a public nuisance.

f. Be free from conditions which are offensive to the sight or smell.

(2) **Appurtenances.** All buildings or structures shall have:

a. Roofs maintained in good repair, free from defects, and free from accumulations of snow, water, or other materials.

b. Gutters and downspouts maintained in good repair.

c. All fascia, soffit, and exterior trim maintained in good repair.

d. Exterior walls maintained in good repair, free from holes, breaks, loose or rotting materials.

e. Porches, decks, and similar appurtenances maintained in good repair and structurally sound.

f. All signs, canopies, awnings, and similar appurtenances maintained in good repair and structurally sound.

(3) **Structural.** All buildings or structures shall have:

a. Foundations maintained in good repair.

b. Floors maintained in good repair.

c. Interior walls maintained in good repair.

d. Ceilings maintained in good repair.

e. All stairs, handrails, and guardrails maintained in good repair and structurally sound.

(4) **Plumbing, electrical, and facilities.** All buildings or structures shall have:

a. All plumbing systems maintained in good repair and in safe and sanitary condition.

b. All electrical systems maintained in good repair and in safe condition.

c. All mechanical facilities maintained in good repair and in safe condition.

(5) **Egress.** All buildings or structures shall have:

a. All required exits maintained clear and unobstructed at all times.

b. All exit doors maintained in good repair and operable.

c. All exit signs maintained in good repair and illuminated.

d. All emergency lighting maintained in good repair and operable.

(6) **Drainage.** All buildings or structures shall have adequate drainage from the roof and premises.
(Code 1993, § 6-71)

#### Sec. 14-122. Buildings not declared unsafe but not in compliance.

Any building which is not in compliance with the standards of this article but which does not meet the criteria for declaration as an unsafe building shall be subject to an order requiring the owner to bring such building into compliance within a reasonable time as determined by the inspector.
(Code 1993, § 6-72)

**Secs. 14-123—14-150. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 3. PROCEDURES

#### Sec. 14-151. Condemnation by inspector.

Whenever the inspector determines that any building or structure is unsafe, he shall report such finding in writing to the town council. The inspector may placard such building as unsafe.
(Code 1993, § 6-81)

#### Sec. 14-152. Removal of condemnation notice.

No person shall remove any condemnation notice posted by the inspector pursuant to this article without written authorization from the inspector.
(Code 1993, § 6-82)

#### Sec. 14-153. Complaint and notice of hearing; lis pendens filing.

The inspector shall cause a complaint and notice of hearing to be served upon the owner of any building determined to be unsafe or not in compliance with this article. The inspector shall file a notice of lis pendens in the office of the register of deeds.
(Code 1993, § 6-83)

#### Sec. 14-154. Order to take corrective action.

Following the hearing, the inspector shall issue an order directing the owner to take such action as is necessary to bring the building into compliance with this article. The order shall allow the owner a minimum of 60 days to comply.
(Code 1993, § 6-84)

#### Sec. 14-155. Service of written notice.

Written notice required under this article shall be served upon the owner personally or by registered or certified mail.
(Code 1993, § 6-85)

#### Sec. 14-156. Appeal to town council.

Any person aggrieved by an order issued pursuant to this article may appeal to the town council within ten days of receipt of the order.
(Code 1993, § 6-86)

#### Sec. 14-157. Failure to comply.

If an owner fails to comply with an order issued pursuant to this article within the time allowed, the inspector may take such action as is authorized by this article to bring the building into compliance or to have it demolished.
(Code 1993, § 6-87)

#### Sec. 14-158. Remedies; lien for demolition costs.

The cost of any remedial action taken by the town pursuant to this article shall constitute a lien upon the real property against which such action was taken. Such lien shall be filed in the office of the register of deeds and shall be collected as unpaid taxes.
(Code 1993, § 6-88)

#### Sec. 14-159. Failure of occupant to comply; vacate.

If an occupant of a building fails to comply with an order to vacate issued pursuant to this article, the inspector may cause summary ejectment proceedings to be initiated.
(Code 1993, § 6-89)

#### Sec. 14-160. Demolition by owner.

Any owner who elects to demolish a building pursuant to this article shall:

(1) Obtain all required permits.

(2) Comply with all applicable state and local regulations regarding demolition.

(3) Remove all debris from the site.

(4) Grade and stabilize the site following demolition.

(5) Restore the site to a clean and safe condition.
(Code 1993, § 6-90)

**Secs. 14-161—14-180. Reserved.**

### DIVISION 4. ADMINISTRATION

#### Sec. 14-181. Inspector's office created; powers.

The office of the nonresidential building maintenance standards inspector is hereby created. The inspector shall have the following powers:

(1) To investigate nonresidential building conditions in the town to determine which buildings are not in compliance with this article.

(2) To enter upon premises for the purpose of making inspections at reasonable times.

(3) To issue and serve complaints, orders, and other papers as necessary to carry out the provisions of this article.

(4) To exercise such other powers as are necessary to carry out the purposes of this article.
(Code 1993, § 6-101)

#### Sec. 14-182. Inspections.

The inspector shall inspect nonresidential buildings as necessary to determine compliance with this article. The inspector shall inspect any building upon receipt of a complaint from any person.
(Code 1993, § 6-102)

#### Sec. 14-183. Defects to be corrected.

Upon inspection of any nonresidential building, the inspector shall note all defects found to be in violation of this article and shall order the owner and occupant to correct all such defects.
(Code 1993, § 6-103)

#### Sec. 14-184. Violations.

Any violation of the provisions of this article by an owner or occupant shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 14-185.
(Code 1993, § 6-104)

#### Sec. 14-185. Penalties.

Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be subject to a civil penalty of $100.00 per violation per day. In addition to or as an alternative to this civil penalty, the town may seek injunctive relief or such other remedies as may be available at law or in equity.
(Code 1993, § 6-105)
