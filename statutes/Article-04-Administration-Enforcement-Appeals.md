# Article 4: Administration, Enforcement, and Appeals

## 160D-401: Application

(a) The provisions of this Article shall apply to all development regulations adopted pursuant to this Chapter. Local governments may apply any of the definitions and procedures authorized by this Chapter to any ordinance adopted under the general police power of cities and counties and may employ any organizational structure, board, commission, or staffing arrangement authorized by this Chapter to any or all aspects of those ordinances. The provisions of this Article also apply to any other local ordinance that substantially affects land use and development.

(b) The provisions of this Article are supplemental to specific provisions included in other Articles of this Chapter. To the extent there are conflicts between the provisions of this Article and the provisions of other Articles of this Chapter, the more specific provisions shall control. This Article does not expand, diminish, or alter the scope of authority for development regulations authorized by other Chapters of the General Statutes.

---

## 160D-402: Administrative Staff

(a) **Authorization.** - Local governments may appoint administrators, inspectors, enforcement officers, planners, technicians, and other staff necessary to develop, administer, and enforce development regulations adopted pursuant to this Chapter.

(b) **Duties.** - Administrative staff duties include:

   - Drafting and implementing plans
   - Determining application completeness
   - Receiving and processing applications for development approvals
   - Providing required notices
   - Making determinations
   - Determining compliance with applicable standards
   - Conducting inspections
   - Issuing or denying certificates of compliance or occupancy
   - Enforcing development regulations, including issuing notices of violation and orders to correct
   - Keeping records of all actions taken
   - Other enforcement actions as authorized

Development regulations may require oaths of office for staff members. Local governments have authority to enact ordinances, administrative procedures, and fee schedules to support the development regulatory program. Building permit administrative and enforcement provisions in Article 11 of this Chapter apply to those permits.

(c) **Alternative Staff Arrangements.** - Local governments may contract with other cities or counties to create joint staff arrangements for enforcing specified laws. Governing boards may designate staff from other jurisdictions with approval. Such designated staff members become agents of the employing government while performing duties within that jurisdiction. One local government may request another to direct staff to exercise powers within its jurisdiction until the request is officially withdrawn pursuant to G.S. 160D-202.

Local governments may contract with individuals, companies, councils of governments, regional planning agencies, metropolitan planning organizations, or rural planning agencies to designate someone who is not a city or county employee to work under local government supervision. Liability for contracted inspections equals liability for inspections performed by employees. Contracted companies or individuals must maintain acceptable errors and omissions and other insurance coverage.

(d) **Financial Support.** - Local governments may appropriate necessary funds and fix reasonable fees for support, administration, and program implementation under this Chapter. Building inspection department fees collected under Article 11 must support that department's administration and operations only. When State Fire Marshal-requested inspections are performed by marketplace pool code-enforcement officials under G.S. 143-151.12(9)a., fees for such inspections must be promptly refunded. This applies to plumbing, electrical systems, general building restrictions, heating and air-conditioning, and general building construction inspections.

## 160D-403: Administrative Development Approvals and Determinations

(a) **Development Approvals.** - No development may commence without required development approval from the local government with jurisdiction. Development approvals must be in writing and may require compliance with applicable State and local laws. Approvals may be issued in print or electronic form; electronic-only approvals must be protected from further editing after issuance. Applications for development approvals may be made by landowners, lessees, persons holding options or contracts to purchase or lease land, authorized agents, or easement holders for development authorized by the easement.

(b) **Determinations and Notice of Determinations.** - Development regulations may designate the staff members authorized to make determinations. The officer making a determination must give written notice to the property owner and the party seeking the determination (if different), delivered by personal delivery, email, or first-class mail to the last address listed on the county tax abstract and to any address provided on the application.

Constructive notice of a determination is conclusively presumed from the date a sign is posted on the property and remains posted for at least 10 days, reading "Zoning Decision" or "Subdivision Decision" or similar language in letters at least six inches high, and identifying contact information for a staff member who can provide information about the determination. Posting is not the only permissible form of notice and is the responsibility of the landowner, applicant, or person requesting the determination; verification of posting must be provided to the responsible staff member. Posting is not required absent an ordinance provision requiring it.

(c) **Duration of Development Approval.** - Unless different periods are specified by this Chapter or other applicable law, development approvals expire one year after issuance if work authorized by the approval has not substantially commenced. Development regulations may provide shorter durations for temporary uses, special events, temporary signs, and similar development, or longer durations for specified types of development. This subsection does not limit vested rights established under G.S. 160D-108 or G.S. 160D-108.1.

(d) **Changes.** - No deviations from the approved application or approval terms are permitted without written approval of proposed changes. Local governments may define by ordinance minor modifications that are eligible for exemption from further review or for administrative approval. Major modifications require the same review and approval process as the original approval.

(e) **Inspections.** - Administrative staff may inspect work undertaken pursuant to a development approval to ensure compliance with applicable State and local laws and the terms of the approval. Staff may enter premises within the jurisdiction at reasonable hours upon presenting proper credentials, with appropriate consent for non-public areas or with inspection warrants. Staff may not require unrestricted written consent to enter property as a condition of accepting applications or issuing development approvals.

(f) **Revocation of Development Approvals.** - In addition to enforcement actions under G.S. 160D-404, development approvals may be revoked by the issuing authority by written notice stating the reasons for revocation. The same review and approval process required for issuance of the approval applies to its revocation, including any required notice or hearings. Development approvals are subject to revocation for:

   - Substantial departure from the approved application, plans, or specifications
   - Refusal or failure to comply with applicable development regulations or delegated State law requirements
   - False statements or misrepresentations made in securing the approval
   - Mistaken issuance in violation of applicable State or local law

Staff revocations may be appealed pursuant to G.S. 160D-405, and the stay provisions in G.S. 160D-405(f) apply if appeals of development regulation enforcement are filed.

(g) **Certificate of Occupancy.** - Upon completion of work authorized by a development approval, local governments may conduct final inspections and issue certificates of compliance or occupancy if the work complies with applicable State and local laws and the terms of the approval. Buildings, structures, or land subject to Article 11 building permits may not be occupied or used until certificates of occupancy or temporary certificates pursuant to G.S. 160D-1116 are issued.

(h) **Optional Communication Requirements.** - Development regulations may require notice to neighboring properties or informational meetings, or both, as part of administrative decision-making processes.

## 160D-404: Enforcement

(a) **Notices of Violation.** - When administrative staff determines that work or activity violates applicable development regulations, other local development regulations, or delegated State law, or violates the terms of a development approval, written notices of violation may be issued. Notices must be delivered to the approval holder and property owner (if different) by personal delivery, electronic delivery, or first-class mail, and may be provided similarly to occupants or persons undertaking the work or activity. Notices may also be posted on the property. The person providing notice must certify delivery to the local government; the certificate is conclusive absent fraud. Except as provided by G.S. 160D-1123 or G.S. 160D-1206, violation notices may be appealed to the board of adjustment pursuant to G.S. 160D-405.

(b) **Stop Work Orders.** - When work or activity subject to this Chapter, other applicable local regulations, or delegated State law substantially violates applicable State or local law or endangers life or property, administrative staff may order the specific violating or hazardous work or activity to stop immediately. Stop work orders must be:

   - In writing
   - Directed to the person doing the work or activity
   - State the specific work or activity to be stopped
   - State the reasons for the order
   - State the conditions under which work or activity may resume

Copies of stop work orders must be delivered to the approval holder and property owner (if different) by personal delivery, electronic delivery, or first-class mail. Persons delivering orders must certify delivery; the certificate is conclusive absent fraud. Except as provided by G.S. 160D-1112 and G.S. 160D-1208, stop work orders may be appealed pursuant to G.S. 160D-405. No further work may proceed in violation of a stop work order pending the ruling on any appeal. Violation of a stop work order constitutes a Class 1 misdemeanor.

(c) **Remedies.**

   (1) Development regulations may be enforced by any remedy provided in G.S. 160A-175 or G.S. 153A-123. When buildings, structures, land, or uses are in violation of this Chapter or regulations adopted under its authority, local governments may institute any appropriate action or proceeding to prevent unlawful erection, construction, alteration, maintenance, use, or development; to restrain, correct, or abate violations; to prevent occupancy of buildings, structures, or land; or to prevent any illegal act, conduct, business, or use.

   (2) When development regulations are applied or enforced outside a city's planning and development regulation jurisdiction as defined in Article 2 of this Chapter, the city and the property owner must certify that the application or enforcement is not coercive or based on withholding of city development approval. Such certification may be evidenced by a signed statement on development approvals.

   (3) When properties included in historic landmarks or historic districts face demolition through deliberate neglect or otherwise, or material alteration, remodeling, removal, or destruction except in compliance with applicable regulations, the local government, historic preservation commission, or any aggrieved party may institute appropriate action to prevent unlawful demolition, destruction, material alteration, or removal; to restrain, correct, or abate violations; or to prevent any illegal act regarding such properties. These remedies are in addition to and supplement any others authorized by this Chapter.

## 160D-405: Appeals of Administrative Decisions

(a) **Appeals.** - Except as provided in G.S. 160D-1403.1, appeals of administrative staff decisions may be taken to the board of adjustment unless a different board is specified by statute or local ordinance. When this function is assigned to boards other than the board of adjustment pursuant to G.S. 160D-302(b), those boards must comply with all procedures applicable to the board of adjustment. Appeals of erosion and sedimentation control, stormwater control, and housing code decisions do not go to the board of adjustment unless required by local ordinance. Appeals of subdivision plat decisions follow procedures in G.S. 160D-1403.

(b) **Standing.** - Any person with standing under G.S. 160D-1402(c) or the local government may appeal to the board. Appeals are filed by submitting written notice to the local government clerk or other official designated by development regulation, stating the grounds for the appeal.

(c) [Repealed by Session Laws 2020-25, s. 10, effective June 19, 2020.]

(d) **Time to Appeal.** - The owner or other party to whom a determination is directed shall have 30 days from receipt of the written notice of determination to file an appeal. Other persons with standing to appeal shall have 30 days from receipt of actual or constructive notice of the determination. Notice by first-class mail is deemed received on the third business day following deposit with the United States Postal Service.

(e) **Record of Decision.** - The official who made the decision being appealed must transmit all documents and exhibits constituting the record of the decision to the board. The official must also provide copies to the appellant and to the property owner (if not the appellant).

(f) **Stays.** - An appeal of a notice of violation or other enforcement order to the board of adjustment, and any subsequent appeal pursuant to G.S. 160D-1402, stays enforcement of the action appealed from and accrual of any fines assessed during the pendency of the appeal or any related civil proceeding. However, if the official who made the decision being appealed certifies to the board that a stay of enforcement would cause imminent peril to life or property or would seriously interfere with enforcement of a development regulation due to the transitory nature of the violation, enforcement of the action shall not be stayed except by a court restraining order. If enforcement is not stayed, the appellant may request an expedited hearing on the appeal, and the board must meet to hear the appeal within 15 days of the request.

Appeals of grants of development approval or affirmations of development regulation consistency do not stay further review of applications for development approval; however, affected parties may request stays of final development approval decisions, including affected building permits.

(g) **Alternative Dispute Resolution.** - The parties to an appeal may agree to mediation or other forms of alternative dispute resolution. Development regulations may establish standards and procedures facilitating voluntary alternative dispute resolution of appeals.

(h) **No Estoppel.** - G.S. 160D-1403.2, which limits local government estoppel defenses, applies to proceedings under this section.

## 160D-406: Quasi-Judicial Procedure

(a) **Process Required.** - Boards shall follow quasi-judicial procedures when making decisions on appeals of administrative determinations, applications for special use permits, certificates of appropriateness, variances, or other quasi-judicial decisions.

(b) **Notice of Hearing.** - Notice of evidentiary hearings must be mailed to the person or entity whose appeal, application, or request is the subject of the hearing; to the owner of the property that is the subject of the hearing (if the owner is not the appellant or applicant); to the owners of all abutting parcels; and to other persons entitled to notice under applicable local development regulations. Local governments may rely on county tax listings to determine ownership. Notices must be mailed at least 10 but not more than 25 days before the hearing. Within this same time period, notices must also be prominently posted on the site or on an adjacent public street or highway right-of-way. A hearing that has been convened may be continued without further advertisement. If a quorum is not present when a hearing is scheduled, the hearing is continued to the next regular meeting without need for additional advertisement.

(c) **Administrative Materials.** - The administrator or staff must transmit all applications, reports, and relevant materials to the board before the hearing. Materials may be distributed to board members before the hearing, provided copies are simultaneously provided to the appellant and to the landowner (if not the appellant). All materials become part of the hearing record and may be provided in written or electronic form. Objections to the inclusion or exclusion of administrative materials may be made before or during the hearing; the board shall rule on any unresolved objections at the hearing.

(d) **Presentation of Evidence.** - The applicant, the local government, and any person with standing under G.S. 160D-1402(c) have the right to participate as parties at the evidentiary hearing. Other witnesses may present competent, material, and substantial evidence that is not repetitive as the board deems appropriate.

Objections regarding jurisdictional and evidentiary issues, including the timeliness of an appeal and the standing of a party, may be made to the board. The chair of the board shall rule on all objections; rulings may be appealed to the full board. These rulings are also subject to judicial review pursuant to G.S. 160D-1402. Objections based on jurisdictional issues may be raised for the first time on judicial review.

(e) **Appearance of Official; New Issues.** - The official who made the decision being appealed, or the person currently holding that position if the official is no longer employed by the local government, must attend the hearing as a witness. The appellant is not limited at the hearing to matters stated in the notice of appeal. If parties or the local government would be unduly prejudiced by the presentation of matters not included in the notice of appeal, the board shall continue the hearing.

(f) **Oaths.** - The chair of the board or any member acting as chair, and the clerk to the board, may administer oaths to witnesses at evidentiary hearings. Any person who, while under oath during a proceeding before a quasi-judicial board, willfully swears falsely is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.

(g) **Subpoenas.** - The chair of the board or any member acting as chair may subpoena witnesses and compel the production of evidence. The applicant, the local government, and any person with standing under G.S. 160D-1402(c) may make written requests for subpoenas, with explanation. The chair shall issue requested subpoenas determined to be relevant, reasonable in nature and scope, and not oppressive. The chair shall rule on any motion to quash or modify a subpoena. Decisions of the chair regarding subpoenas may be immediately appealed to the full board. If any person fails or refuses to obey a subpoena issued pursuant to this subsection, the board or the party seeking compliance may apply to the General Court of Justice for an order requiring compliance; the court has jurisdiction to issue such orders after proper notice to all parties.

(h) **Appeals in Nature of Certiorari.** - Hearings on appeals pursuant to G.S. 160D-947(e) or other appeals in the nature of certiorari shall be based on the record below, and the scope of review shall be as provided in G.S. 160D-1402(j).

(i) **Voting.** - A four-fifths vote of the board members shall be required to grant a variance. A simple majority of the board members shall be required to decide any other quasi-judicial matter or to determine an appeal made in the nature of certiorari. Vacant positions on the board and members who are disqualified from voting on a quasi-judicial matter pursuant to G.S. 160D-109(d) shall not be counted when calculating the required number of votes if no qualified alternate is available to take the place of the disqualified or absent member.

(j) **Decisions.** - The board must determine the contested facts and make its decision within a reasonable time. When hearing appeals, the board may reverse or affirm, wholly or partly, or may modify the decision being appealed and shall make any order, requirement, decision, or determination that ought to be made. The board has all the powers of the official who made the decision being appealed.

Every quasi-judicial decision must be based on competent, material, and substantial evidence in the record. Each decision must be in writing, reflect the board's determination of contested facts and their application to applicable standards, be approved by the board, and be signed by the chair or other duly authorized member of the board. A decision is effective upon filing with the clerk to the board or such other office or official as the development regulation specifies.

The decision must be delivered to the applicant, landowner, and any person who has submitted a written request for a copy within a reasonable time by personal delivery, electronic mail, or first-class mail. Certifications of notice are conclusive absent fraud.

(k) **Judicial Review.** - Every quasi-judicial decision is subject to review by the superior court by proceedings in the nature of certiorari pursuant to G.S. 160D-1402. Any petition for review must be filed within the time period specified in G.S. 160D-1405(d). Local government governing boards may settle litigation subject to Article 33C of Chapter 143 of the General Statutes.
