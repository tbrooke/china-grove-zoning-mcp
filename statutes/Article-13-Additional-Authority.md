# Article 13: Additional Authority

## Part 1. Open Space Acquisition

### 160D-1301: Legislative Intent

The General Assembly intends to enable local governments to acquire real property interests to preserve, through limitation of their future use, open spaces and areas for public use and enjoyment. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1302: Finding of Necessity

The General Assembly finds that rapid growth and spread of urban development is encroaching upon, or eliminating, many open areas and spaces of varied size and character, including many having significant scenic or esthetic values, which areas and spaces if preserved and maintained in their present open state would constitute important physical, social, esthetic, or economic assets to existing or impending urban development. It is declared that the acquisition of interests or rights in real property for the preservation of open spaces and areas constitutes a public purpose for which public funds may be expended or advanced. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1303: Local Governments Authorized to Acquire and Reconvey Real Property

Any local government may acquire the fee or any lesser interest, development right, easement, covenant, or other contractual right of or to real property necessary to achieve the purposes of this Part. The local government may reconvey property back to original owners under restrictive covenants, but to no other person by private sale. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1304: Joint Action by Governing Bodies

A local government may enter into any agreement with any other local government for the purpose of jointly exercising the authority granted by this Part. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1305: Powers of Governing Bodies

A local government exercising authority under this Part may:

(1) Enter into and carry out contracts with the State or federal government or any agencies thereof under which grants or other assistance are made.

(2) Accept any assistance or funds that may be granted by the State or federal government with or without a contract.

(3) Agree to and comply with any reasonable conditions imposed upon grants.

(4) Make expenditures from any funds so granted.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1306: Appropriations Authorized

For the purposes set forth in this Part, a local government may appropriate funds not otherwise limited as to use by law. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1307: Definitions

As used in this Part:

(1) Open space or open area. - Any space or area characterized by great natural scenic beauty or where the existing openness, natural condition, or present state of use would enhance the present or potential value of abutting or surrounding urban development. The term includes interests or rights in real property and open space land or uses.

(2) Open space land or open space uses. - Any undeveloped or predominantly undeveloped land in an urban area with value for:

a. Park and recreational purposes.

b. Conservation of land and other natural resources.

c. Historic or scenic purposes.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1308 through 160D-1310

Reserved for future codification purposes. (2019-111, s. 2.4.)

## Part 2. Community Development and Redevelopment

### 160D-1311: Community Development Programs and Activities

**(a)** A local government may engage in and appropriate funds for community development programs, including:

(1) Programs of assistance and financing of rehabilitation of private buildings principally for the benefit of low- and moderate-income persons, or for the restoration or preservation of older neighborhoods.

(2) Programs concerned with employment, economic development, crime prevention, child care, health, drug abuse, education, and welfare needs of persons of low and moderate income.

**(b)** Governing boards may exercise powers granted to redevelopment commissions and housing authorities. Boards may delegate these responsibilities, contract with other entities, or undertake activities for other governments.

**(c)** A local government may create one or more advisory committees to advise it and to make recommendations concerning the programs or activities.

**(d)** A governing board may submit loan guaranty programs to voters for approval via referendum, conducted under applicable election laws.

**(e)** Governments may receive and dispense funds from the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) Section 108 Loan Guarantee program. Governments receiving funds directly may pledge current and future CDBG funds for loan guarantees. Such pledges are not a debt or liability of the State or any political subdivision of the State.

**(f)** Cities and counties in economically distressed counties may retain program income from Small Cities Community Development Block Grants for local economic development revolving loan funds supporting activities eligible under the federal Housing and Community Development Act.

**(g)** No State or local taxes shall be appropriated or expended by a county pursuant to this section for any purpose not expressly authorized by G.S. 153A-149, unless the purpose is first submitted to a vote.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d); 2025-25, s. 25.)

### 160D-1312: Acquisition and Disposition of Property for Redevelopment

Local governments may exercise the following powers without Urban Redevelopment Law compliance:

(1) Acquire real property meeting criteria including:

a. Blighted, deteriorated, deteriorating, undeveloped, or inappropriately developed from the standpoint of sound community development.

b. Appropriate for rehabilitation or conservation activities.

c. Appropriate for housing construction or the economic development of the community.

d. Appropriate for the preservation or restoration of historic sites, the beautification of urban land, the conservation of open spaces, natural resources, and scenic areas, or the guidance of urban development.

(2) Clear, demolish, remove, or rehabilitate buildings and improvements on land so acquired.

(3) Retain property so acquired for public purposes, or to dispose, through sale, lease, or otherwise in accordance with Article 12 of Chapter 160A or G.S. 160A-514.

(4) Sell or transfer property at private sale for residential, recreational, commercial, industrial, or other uses after governing board approval and legislative hearing. Property must be appraised, and the consideration for the conveyance shall not be less than the appraised value.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, ss. 43, 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1313: Urban Development Action Grants

Local governments may contract to undertake activities furthering Urban Development Action Grants purposes, including the authority to enter into and carry out contracts or agreements to extend loans, loan subsidies, or grants.

Local act references to "Urban Development Action Grants" shall be construed to refer to continuation programs by whatever designation. (2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1314: Urban Homesteading Programs

Local governments may establish urban homesteading programs conveying residential property of little or no value to persons rehabilitating it. Property qualifies if the cost of bringing the property into compliance with the local government's housing code exceeds sixty percent (60%) of the property's appraised value.

A local government may:

(1) Acquire by purchase, gift, or otherwise, but not eminent domain, residential property for the program or transfer previously acquired property.

(2) Convey residential property by private sale for nominal monetary consideration to persons who qualify as grantees.

(3) Convey property subject to conditions:

a. A requirement that the grantee shall use the property as the grantee's principal place of residence for a minimum number of years.

b. A requirement that the grantee rehabilitate the property so that it meets or exceeds minimum housing code standards.

c. A requirement that the grantee maintain insurance on the property.

d. Any other specific conditions, including design standards.

e. A provision for the termination of the grantee's interest in the property and its reversion upon condition failure.

(4) Subordinate the local government's interest in the property to any security interest granted by the grantee to a lender.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1315: Downtown Development Projects

**(a) Definition.** A "downtown development project" or "joint development project" is a capital project, in a central business district, comprising one or more buildings and including both public and private facilities.

**(b) Authorization.** If the governing board finds the project will have a significant effect on the revitalization of the jurisdiction, the local government may acquire, construct, own, and operate or participate in such projects. Contracts may specify:

(1) The property interests of both the local government and the developer or developers in the project, provided that the property interests of the local government shall be limited to facilities for a public purpose.

(2) The responsibilities of the local government and the developer or developers for construction of the project.

(3) The responsibilities with respect to financing the project.

**(c) Eligible Property.** Projects may be constructed on property acquired by the developer or developers, on property directly acquired by the local government, or on property acquired by the local government while exercising the powers of a redevelopment commission.

**(d) Conveyance of Property Rights.** The local government may convey property interests, including air rights, as follows:

(1) If acquired as redevelopment commission, convey pursuant to the Urban Redevelopment Law or any local modification thereof.

(2) If directly acquired, convey pursuant to G.S. 160D-1312, and Article 12 of Chapter 160A does not apply.

(3) In lieu of conveying the fee interest in air rights, the local government may convey a leasehold interest for a period not to exceed 99 years.

**(e) Construction.** Contracts may specify developers construct the entire project. Contracts shall include such provisions as the governing board deems sufficient to ensure public facilities meet needs and are constructed at a reasonable price. Projects are exempt from Article 8 of Chapter 143 if local government funds constitute no more than fifty percent (50%) of the total costs.

**(f) Operation.** Governments may contract for the operation of any public facility or facilities by a person, partnership, firm, or corporation, public or private, with provisions ensuring operation for the benefit of the citizens of the local government.

**(g) Grant Funds.** To assist in the financing of its share of a joint development project, the local government may apply for, accept, and expend grant funds from the federal or state governments.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

### 160D-1316: Low- and Moderate-Income Housing Programs

Any local government is authorized to:

(1) Engage in and appropriate and expend funds for residential housing construction, new or rehabilitated, for sale or rental to persons and families of low and moderate income.

(2) Acquire real property by voluntary purchase from the owners to be developed by the local government or to be used by the local government to provide affordable housing.

(3) Convey property by private sale to any public or private entity that provides affordable housing with covenants or conditions that assure the property will be developed for sale or lease to persons of low or moderate income.

(4) Convey residential property by private sale to persons of low or moderate income, in accordance with procedures and standards established by the local government and with any terms and conditions that the governing board may determine.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)

## Part 3. Miscellaneous

### 160D-1320: Program to Finance Energy Improvements

**(a) Purpose.** The General Assembly finds it is in the best interest of the citizens of North Carolina to promote and encourage renewable energy and energy efficiency to conserve energy and expand employment. Local governments have an integral role in this purpose and may establish programs financing renewable energy or energy efficiency improvements permanently affixed to residential, commercial, or other real property.

**(b) Financing Assistance.** Local governments may:

- Establish a revolving loan fund and a loan loss reserve fund for financing renewable energy or efficiency improvements.

- Establish other local government energy efficiency finance programs funded through federal grants.

- Use State and federal grants and loans and its general revenue for this financing.

- Charge interest at not exceeding eight percent (8%) per annum, excluding other fees.

- Originate loans with term not greater than 20 years.

**(c) Definition.** "Renewable energy source" has the same meaning as "renewable energy resource" in G.S. 62-133.8.

(2019-111, s. 2.4; 2020-3, s. 4.33(a); 2020-25, s. 51(a), (b), (d).)
