# Article 6: Development Regulation

## 160D-601: Procedure for Adopting, Amending, or Repealing Development Regulations

(a) **Hearing with Published Notice.** - Before adopting, amending, or repealing any ordinance or development regulation authorized by this Chapter, the governing board shall hold a legislative hearing. Notice of the hearing shall be published once a week for two successive calendar weeks in a newspaper having general circulation in the area. The first publication of the notice shall occur not less than 10 days nor more than 25 days before the date scheduled for the hearing, excluding the day of publication but including the day of the hearing.

(b) **Notice to Military Bases.** - When a development regulation modification affects the zoning map or the permitted uses within an area that is within five miles of the perimeter boundary of a military base, the local government shall send written notice of the proposed changes by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the commander of the military base not less than 10 days nor more than 25 days before the date fixed for the hearing. The governing board shall consider any compatibility comments from the military commander before making a final determination on the proposed modification.

(c) **Ordinance Required.** - Development regulations adopted pursuant to this Chapter shall be adopted by ordinance.

(d) **Down-Zoning.** - No down-zoning amendment to a zoning regulation shall be initiated by the local government or its agencies nor approved by the governing board without the written consent of all property owners whose property is the subject of the down-zoning amendment. Down-zoning encompasses:

   - Decreasing development density below levels previously allowed
   - Reducing the types of permitted uses
   - Creating nonconformities on non-residential land

(e) **Withdrawn or Denied Applications.** - Development regulations shall not impose mandatory waiting periods that prevent resubmission of denied or withdrawn applications for zoning map amendments, zoning text amendments, development applications, or development approval requests.

## 160D-602: Notice of Hearing on Proposed Zoning Map Amendments

(a) **Mailed Notice.** - Ordinances shall govern zoning regulations and the determination, amendment, supplementation, and change of zoning district boundaries pursuant to this Chapter. The owners of affected parcels and the owners of all abutting parcels shall receive mailed notice of the hearing on a proposed zoning map amendment by first-class mail at the last addresses listed for such owners on the county tax abstracts. Properties are considered abutting even if separated by a street, railroad, or other transportation corridor. Notice shall be deposited in the mail at least 10 but not more than 25 days prior to the date of the hearing.

When zoning map amendments are proposed concurrently with an expansion of a municipality's extraterritorial jurisdiction under G.S. 160D-202, a single hearing may be held with combined notice mailed at least 30 days prior to the hearing.

(b) **Optional Notice for Large-Scale Zoning Map Amendments.** - When a proposed zoning map amendment affects more than 50 properties owned by at least 50 different owners, the local government may forgo individual first-class mail notice and instead publish notice as required by G.S. 160D-601, with the additional requirement that the published advertisements be not less than one-half of a newspaper page in size. Property owners outside the newspaper's circulation area must still receive mailed notice pursuant to subsection (a).

(c) **Posted Notice.** - A notice of the proposed amendment shall be prominently posted on the site of the proposed amendment, on an adjacent public street, or on a highway right-of-way within the same time period specified for mailed notices. When multiple parcels are involved, multiple postings are not necessary if sufficient notices are posted to provide reasonable notice to interested persons.

(d) [Repealed by Session Laws 2020-25, s. 13, effective June 19, 2020.]

(e) **Optional Communication Requirements.** - Zoning regulations may require communication by the proposer of a zoning map amendment to neighboring property owners and residents and may require that the proposer report on such communications to the local government.

## 160D-603: Citizen Comments

Zoning regulations may be amended, supplemented, changed, modified, or repealed. When residents or property owners submit a written statement regarding a proposed amendment, modification, or repeal to a zoning regulation, including a text or map amendment, to the clerk of the governing board at least two business days prior to the proposed vote on the amendment, the clerk must deliver those statements to all members of the governing board. For quasi-judicial proceedings under G.S. 160D-705, the clerk shall provide only the names and addresses of persons who have submitted comments, and the provision of such information shall not disqualify any member of the board from voting on the matter.

## 160D-604: Planning Board Review and Comment

(a) **Initial Zoning.** - A local government exercising the power to zone for the first time must create or designate a planning board. The planning board must prepare or review the proposed zoning regulation, including the full text and maps showing proposed district boundaries. The planning board may hold public meetings and legislative hearings during the preparation period. Upon completion, the planning board must make a written recommendation regarding adoption of the regulation to the governing board. The governing board may not hold its required legislative hearing or take action on the proposed regulation until it has received the planning board's recommendation. After its hearing, the governing board may refer the regulation back to the planning board for further recommendations before taking final action.

(b) **Zoning Amendments.** - After initial adoption of zoning regulations, all proposed zoning amendments shall be submitted to the planning board for review and comment. If the planning board does not submit a report within 30 days of referral, the governing board may proceed without the planning board's report. The governing board is not bound by the recommendations of the planning board.

(c) **Review of Other Ordinances and Actions.** - Development regulations other than zoning regulations may be referred to the planning board for review and comment. Development regulation ordinances may require that proposed future amendments be submitted to the planning board for review and comment. Other actions taken pursuant to this Chapter may also be referred to the planning board for review and comment.

(d) **Plan Consistency.** - When reviewing proposed zoning text or map amendments, the planning board shall advise and comment on whether the proposed action is consistent with any comprehensive or land-use plan that has been adopted and any other officially adopted plan that is applicable. The planning board shall provide a written recommendation to the governing board that addresses plan consistency and any other relevant matters. A planning board statement that a proposed amendment is inconsistent with an adopted plan shall not preclude consideration or approval of the proposed amendment by the governing board. For large-scale rezonings subject to G.S. 160D-602(b), the planning board statement may address the overall rezoning and the relationship of the rezoning to the plan.

(e) **Separate Board Required.** - The planning board review and comment function required by this section may not be assigned to the governing board itself and must be performed by a separate planning board or other board assigned that function.

## 160D-605: Governing Board Statement

(a) **Plan Consistency.** - When adopting or rejecting any zoning text or map amendment, the governing board shall approve a brief statement describing whether its action is consistent or inconsistent with an adopted comprehensive or land-use plan. This requirement is considered satisfied when the minutes of the governing board meeting reflect that the board considered the planning board's recommendations and any relevant provisions of an adopted comprehensive or land-use plan. When an amendment that is adopted is deemed inconsistent with an adopted plan, the zoning amendment has the effect of also amending any future land-use map in the approved plan, and no additional request or application for a plan amendment is required. Plan consistency statements are not subject to judicial review. For large-scale rezonings, the governing board statement may address the overall rezoning and the relationship of the rezoning to the plan.

(b) **Additional Reasonableness Statement for Rezonings.** - When adopting or rejecting any petition for a zoning map amendment, the governing board shall also approve a statement analyzing the reasonableness of the proposed rezoning. The reasonableness statement may consider, among other factors:

   - The size, physical conditions, and other attributes of the area proposed for rezoning
   - The benefits and detriments to the landowners, neighbors, and the surrounding community
   - The relationship between the current actual and permissible development on the tract and adjoining areas and the development that would be permissible under the proposed amendment
   - Why the action taken is in the public interest
   - Any changed conditions warranting the amendment

For large-scale rezonings, the reasonableness statement may address the overall rezoning.

(c) **Single Statement Permissible.** - The reasonableness statement required by subsection (b) and the plan consistency statement required by subsection (a) may be approved as a single statement.
