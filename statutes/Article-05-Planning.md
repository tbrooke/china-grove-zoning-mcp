# Article 5: Planning

## 160D-501: Plans

(a) **Requirements for Zoning.** - As a condition of adopting and applying zoning regulations under this Chapter, a local government shall adopt and reasonably maintain a comprehensive plan or land-use plan.

(a1) **Plans.** - A comprehensive plan sets forth goals, policies, and programs intended to guide the present and future physical, social, and economic development of the jurisdiction. A land-use plan uses text and maps to designate the future use or reuse of land.

Planning documentation may evaluate existing conditions and future trends regarding demographics, economics, environment, and cultural aspects. The planning process shall incorporate citizen participation in the preparation and adoption of plans.

Local governments may create additional plans including small area plans, neighborhood plans, hazard mitigation plans, transportation plans, housing plans, and recreation and open space plans.

(b) **Comprehensive Plan Contents.** - A comprehensive plan may address:

   (1) Issues and opportunities, including trends, citizen values, community vision, and growth principles.

   (2) Desired growth patterns and civic design, covering land use location, distribution, characteristics, urban form, utilities, and transportation networks.

   (3) Employment opportunities, economic development, and community development.

   (4) Acceptable public services and infrastructure levels supporting development, including water, waste disposal, utilities, emergency services, transportation, education, recreation, and financing plans.

   (5) Housing with varied types and affordability for all persons and income levels.

   (6) Recreation and open spaces.

   (7) Mitigation of natural hazards including flooding, winds, wildfires, and unstable lands.

   (8) Environmental and natural resource protection, including agricultural and mineral resources, water and air quality.

   (9) Protection of significant architectural, scenic, cultural, historical, or archaeological resources.

   (10) Implementation measures analysis including regulations, public investments, and educational programs.

(c) **Adoption and Effect of Plans.** - Plans shall be adopted by the governing board with the advice and consultation of the planning board.

Plan adoption represents a legislative decision and shall follow the procedures for zoning text amendments under G.S. 160D-601. Plans adopted under this Chapter are advisory in nature without independent regulatory effect.

Plans adopted under this Chapter shall be considered by the planning board and governing board when considering proposed amendments to zoning regulations.

If a zoning amendment deems a plan amended through G.S. 160D-605, that amendment shall be noted in the plan. However, plans requiring review under G.S. 113A-110 become effective only after such review completion.

## 160D-502: Grants, Contracts, and Technical Assistance

(a) **Grants and Services.** - A local government may accept, receive, and disburse in furtherance of its functions any funds, grants, and services made available by the federal government and its agencies, the State government and its agencies, any local government and its agencies, and any private and civic sources.

Local governments may contract with State and federal governments for planning assistance under reasonable conditions.

(b) **Contracts.** - Local governments may contract with other cities, counties, regional councils, planning agencies, or private consultants to furnish or receive technical planning assistance.

(c) **Appropriations, Compensation, and Financing.** - A local government is authorized to make appropriations that may be necessary to carry out activities or contracts authorized by this Article or to support and compensate members of a planning board, and may levy taxes for these purposes.

---

## 160D-503: Coordination of Planning

A local government may undertake any of the planning activities authorized by this Article in coordination with other local governments, State agencies, or regional agencies created under Article 19 of Chapter 153A of the General Statutes or Article 20 of Chapter 160A of the General Statutes.
