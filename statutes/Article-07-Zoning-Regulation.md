# Article 7: Zoning Regulation

## 160D-701: Purposes

Zoning regulations shall be made in accordance with a comprehensive plan and shall be designed to promote the public health, safety, and general welfare. To that end, regulations may be adopted and applied to accomplish the following permitted regulatory purposes:

- Providing adequate light and air
- Preventing the overcrowding of land
- Avoiding undue concentration of population
- Lessening congestion in the streets
- Securing safety from fire, panic, and dangers
- Facilitating the efficient and adequate provision of transportation, water, sewerage, schools, parks, and other public requirements
- Conserving the value of buildings and property

Regulations shall be made with reasonable consideration, among other things, as to the character of the district and its peculiar suitability for particular uses, and with a view to conserving the value of buildings and encouraging the most appropriate use of land throughout the jurisdiction. Road facility service levels shall not be used as the basis for denying a request to rezone property for school uses.

---

## 160D-702: Grant of Power

(a) For the purpose of providing for the public health, safety, and general welfare, local governments may adopt zoning regulations including, but not limited to, regulations controlling:

   - The height, number of stories, and size of buildings and other structures
   - The percentage of a lot that may be occupied
   - The size of yards, courts, and other open spaces
   - The density of population
   - The location and use of buildings, structures, and land for trade, industry, residence, or other purposes

Development over estuarine waters and over navigable waters is also subject to local zoning regulation. Zoning regulations may also provide for the dedication of rights-of-way for streets and utilities and the dedication of recreational space.

(b) **Building Design Elements.** - Zoning regulations shall not regulate building design elements for structures subject to regulation under the North Carolina Residential Code for One- and Two-Family Dwellings except in the following circumstances:

   - Structures located in a designated historic district or designated as a landmark pursuant to Part 4 of Article 9 of this Chapter
   - Requirements necessary to comply with safety codes adopted under the police power
   - Requirements necessary to maintain the manufactured housing consistency provisions of G.S. 160D-910
   - Requirements necessary to participate in the National Flood Insurance Program

(c) **Prohibited Regulations.** - Zoning regulations shall not include any of the following:

   - Minimum square footage requirements for residential structures
   - Parking space size requirements exceeding 9 feet wide by 20 feet long, except for handicapped, parallel, or diagonal parking
   - Fire apparatus access road requirements exceeding those set by the fire code
   - Pavement standards exceeding Department of Transportation minimum requirements

---

## 160D-703: Zoning Districts

(a) **Types of Districts.** - For any zoning regulation adopted pursuant to this Article, a local government may create conventional, conditional, form-based, and overlay zoning districts, as well as other types of zoning districts as may be appropriate. Conditional district regulations shall require the petitioner's written consent to the conditions and shall not include conditions that the local government is otherwise prohibited from imposing, including unauthorized taxes, impact fees, or building design elements not otherwise authorized by statute.

(b1) **Affordable Housing.** - Zoning regulations shall not include requirements that a development be in harmony with surrounding areas for affordable housing developments that include units for households with incomes below eighty percent (80%) of the area median income.

(b) **Uniformity.** - Regulations shall be uniform for each class of zoning district within the jurisdiction, though standards may vary between different types of districts. Development standards may also be adopted and applied jurisdiction-wide without regard to zoning district boundaries.

---

## 160D-704: Incentives

Local governments may grant density bonuses or other adjustments to development requirements for buildings constructed to meet energy efficiency standards recognized by nationally recognized certification programs. Local governments may reduce building permit fees for buildings that achieve LEED certification, Green Globes ratings, or equivalent nationally-recognized environmental design certifications.

---

## 160D-705: Quasi-Judicial Zoning Decisions

(a) Boards designated by development regulations may hear and decide quasi-judicial zoning decisions, including appeals of administrative enforcement decisions and applications for special use permits, following the procedures specified in applicable statutes.

(b) **Special Use Permits.** - In granting special use permits, the board may impose reasonable and appropriate conditions and safeguards, including requirements for the dedication of rights-of-way for streets and utilities and the dedication of recreational facilities. However, conditions shall not include requirements that the local government is otherwise prohibited from imposing, including unauthorized taxes, impact fees, or building design elements not otherwise authorized by statute.

(c) **Variances.** - When unnecessary hardships would result from the strict application of a zoning regulation, the board of adjustment may grant a variance from the regulation upon finding and recording in its decision all of the following:

   (1) Unnecessary hardship would result from the strict application of the regulation, based on conditions peculiar to the property rather than personal circumstances or self-created conditions.

   (2) The hardship stems from conditions peculiar and unique to the property, such as its location, size, or topography.

   (3) The hardship is not the result of the applicant's own actions.

   (4) The requested variance is consistent with the spirit, purpose, and intent of the regulation, will secure public safety and welfare, and will do substantial justice.

---

## 160D-706: Zoning Conflicts

Whenever the regulations made under authority of this Article require a greater width or size of yards, courts, or other open spaces, require a lower height of buildings or smaller number of stories, require a greater percentage of lot to be left unoccupied, or impose other higher standards than are required in any other statute or local ordinance or regulation, the regulations made under authority of this Article shall govern. Whenever the provisions of any other statute or local ordinance or regulation require a greater width or size of yards, courts, or other open spaces, require a lower height of buildings or a smaller number of stories, require a greater percentage of lot to be left unoccupied, or impose other higher standards than are required by the regulations made under authority of this Article, the provisions of that statute or local ordinance or regulation shall govern.

Local definitions of the terms "building," "dwelling," "mobile home," "manufactured home," or similar terms shall be consistent with the definitions used in State statutes and agency rules.
